GPT-5's breakthrough in theoretical physics represents a qualitative leap beyond productivity gains, with the model reproducing a top researcher's hardest paper in 30 minutes and solving year-long physics problems before an expert's plane could land. Alex Lupsasca's "vibe physics" experiments show AI generating novel 110-page research papers with original techniques, marking the first clear example of AI extending the frontier of human knowledge rather than just optimizing existing workflows.

---

- **Jagged Frontier Effect** GPT-5 shows only moderate improvements for routine tasks like email writing, but dramatic capability jumps for cutting-edge scientific reasoning, explaining mixed public reception despite major advances

- **Reproduction Speed** Alex Lupsasca's most challenging physics paper, which took extensive time to develop, was reproduced by GPT-5 in 30 minutes using a textbook priming technique suggested by OpenAI's CRO

- **Novel Problem Solving** ChatGPT solved a year-long theoretical physics problem about gluon amplitudes in one week, finding an elegant solution through the "half-collinear regime" approach that experts had missed

- **Technique Innovation** The model didn't just map existing solutions but imported new mathematical techniques specific to graviton physics when extending gluon research, demonstrating genuine methodological creativity

- **Knowledge Generation** "Vibe physics" produced 110 pages of novel quantum gravity research in three days, verified over three weeks, representing true frontier extension rather than recombination of known results

- **Scope Amplification** AI's ability to output validated results at conception speed allows individual theorists to tackle far more ambitious problems by rapidly exploring multiple research avenues

- **Domain Expertise Gap** Physics breakthroughs remain invisible to general users focused on productivity tasks, creating disconnect between transformative capabilities and public perception of AI progress
