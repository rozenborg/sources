Onyx Security has developed an AI control plane that monitors and secures autonomous agents by understanding their intent and context rather than just their outputs, addressing a critical gap as enterprises deploy increasingly powerful AI systems that could manage critical infrastructure. The company's approach involves training specialized models to interpret agent behavior and provide vendor-independent oversight, positioning itself as essential infrastructure for the coming wave of autonomous AI deployment.

---

- **AI Control Plane Architecture** - Onyx creates a supervisory layer that monitors AI agent permissions, contexts, and intentions in real-time, balancing security needs with latency and cost requirements for enterprise deployment

- **Intent-Based Monitoring** - Current security controls focus on outputs rather than understanding what agents are trying to accomplish, creating blind spots that Onyx addresses through specialized models that interpret agent reasoning and goals

- **Vendor-Independent Oversight** - The platform provides security monitoring across different AI model providers, preventing enterprises from being locked into single-vendor security solutions as they deploy multiple AI systems

- **Enterprise Deployment Reality** - Large enterprises are moving cautiously with AI agent deployment due to security concerns, creating demand for robust oversight systems before wider adoption of autonomous capabilities

- **Proprietary Model Training** - Onyx trains its own models specifically for security monitoring rather than relying on general-purpose LLMs, enabling more effective detection of malicious or unintended agent behavior

- **Critical Infrastructure Risk** - As AI agents become capable enough to manage power grids and water supplies, the stakes for preventing rogue behavior increase dramatically, making enterprise-grade security controls essential

- **Phased Rollout Strategy** - The company is taking a gradual approach to deploying its most advanced capabilities, reflecting the high-stakes nature of AI security in enterprise environments
