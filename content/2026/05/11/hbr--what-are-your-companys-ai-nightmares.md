Companies are repeatedly retrofitting outdated AI risk management frameworks built for simpler technologies, creating a reactive cycle that leaves organizations vulnerable as they scramble to update ethics-based policies for each new AI breakthrough rather than building adaptable governance systems.

---

- **Reactive Framework Problem** - Organizations built AI governance around pre-2022 traditional AI, then hastily updated for generative AI, and now face another scramble for AI agents, creating policy lag behind technological reality

- **Ethics-Centric Limitation** - Current programs focus heavily on writing ethical principles and policies rather than developing operational risk management capabilities that can adapt to evolving AI technologies

- **Executive-Led Blind Spots** - Top-down policy development may miss ground-level risks and implementation challenges that emerge with each new AI capability wave

- **Update Fatigue Risk** - Constant program revisions for each AI advancement could lead to governance fatigue, inconsistent implementation, and reduced organizational buy-in

- **Adaptability Gap** - Most frameworks lack built-in flexibility to handle unknown future AI capabilities, suggesting need for principle-based rather than technology-specific governance approaches

- **Operational Disconnect** - Focus on enterprise-wide policies may not translate effectively to practical risk management at the team and project level where AI implementation actually occurs
