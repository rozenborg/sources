Opendoor's new CEO Kaz Nejatian claims the struggling iBuyer has reversed course after correcting two critical strategic mistakes: excessive risk aversion that hurt margins and warehousing inventory during a slow market. The company has dramatically restructured operations by replacing 11-person transaction teams with single AI-powered employees and launched a new agent partnership program to embed its services directly into realtor workflows.

---

- **Operational turnaround metrics** - Revenue, conversion rates, and margins have increased monthly since September 2024, though specific numbers weren't disclosed

- **Radical workforce restructuring** - Reduced transaction processing from 11 employees per deal to just one person, with AI now handling most internal work processes

- **Strategic pivot to agent partnerships** - Launched "Cash Now, More Later" product integrated into agent workflows through RealScout partnership, offering 1-2% bonus commissions to referring agents

- **Leadership philosophy shift** - CEO embraces "founder mode" over operational management, prioritizing outcomes over processes and acknowledging the company "not everyone would want to work at"

- **AI-first transformation** - Positions as "AI maximalist" strategy, viewing artificial intelligence as employment creator rather than job destroyer for the proptech sector

- **Weekly operational cadence** - Abandoned quarterly thinking for weekly decision cycles, shipping and measuring performance on seven-day intervals rather than traditional reporting periods

- **Stock performance context** - Share price recovered from near-delisting to meme stock territory in 2024, though CEO dismisses short-term fluctuations as irrelevant to fundamental business health
