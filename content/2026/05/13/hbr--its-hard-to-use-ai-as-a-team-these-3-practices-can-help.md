Organizations risk fragmenting their teams and degrading meeting quality as AI adoption in collaborative settings is expected to triple over the next three years, despite executive optimism about improved outcomes. Without proper preparation, AI integration can inadvertently reduce participation and diminish team ownership of decisions.

---

- **Executive expectations** 500 global executives surveyed by Capgemini anticipate active AI use in team meetings will more than triple within three years, with leaders expecting better meeting quality and outcomes

- **Integration challenge** Research indicates AI doesn't naturally integrate into team environments, contradicting assumptions that adoption will be seamless or automatic

- **Participation risk** Introducing AI tools without groundwork can narrow participation among team members, potentially silencing voices or creating hierarchies based on AI familiarity

- **Discussion fragmentation** Poorly implemented AI can break up meeting flow and coherent discussion threads, making it harder to reach consensus or maintain focus

- **Ownership dilution** Teams may shift decision-making responsibility away from human participants toward AI recommendations, reducing accountability and team buy-in

- **Preparation imperative** Organizations need structured approaches and foundational work before deploying AI in collaborative settings to avoid these pitfalls
