Major corporations across hotels, grocery, and real estate are facing federal antitrust actions for AI-driven price coordination they never intended, revealing a new competitive trap where independent AI systems trained on similar data automatically converge on identical strategies. This "Agentic Convergence Trap" eliminates competitive differentiation not through communication or collusion, but through machine learning reaching the same logical conclusions at inhuman speed.

---

- **Economic evidence is definitive** German gas stations saw 38% margin increases when competing firms both adopted AI pricing, while U.S. airline AI systems converge on fares faster than human strategies can differentiate, reducing consumer welfare despite expanding output.

- **Vendor switching won't solve it** The root cause isn't shared software but shared learning processes operating at machine speed on similar data, meaning proprietary platforms will still converge if they optimize similar objectives using comparable market signals.

- **Governance failure, not technology failure** Executive teams accept platform defaults under competitive pressure, eliminate human review processes for efficiency, then watch AI systems independently learn their way to market-wide coordination with no individual decision-maker choosing that outcome.

- **Strategic variation requires deliberate design** Winners will optimize AI for objectives beyond platform defaults (like Starbucks optimizing visit frequency vs. transaction size), feed systems proprietary data competitors cannot access, and maintain human oversight for decisions where AI convergence would eliminate competitive advantage.

- **New metrics needed urgently** Track decision correlation with competitors, timing overlap of AI-initiated moves, and data exclusivity ratios—treating declining divergence as seriously as falling customer satisfaction since convergence creates antitrust liability without executive intent.

- **Boardroom accountability essential** The question isn't whether your AI works, but whether it's working for you or quietly optimizing the entire market, requiring executives to explicitly decide which decisions must preserve strategic variation regardless of AI recommendations.
