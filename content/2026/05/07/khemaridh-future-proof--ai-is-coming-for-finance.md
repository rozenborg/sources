Anthropic just released ten open-source AI agent templates targeting the most time-consuming work in financial services, causing FactSet shares to plummet 8.1% and Morningstar to fall 3% while broader markets climbed 1%. These aren't just theoretical tools—they're ready-to-deploy templates that can build pitch decks, prep meetings, review earnings, and maintain financial models, all built from simple text files that non-coders can customize.

---

- **Market reaction signals disruption threat** - Financial data companies saw immediate stock declines while Anthropic announced partnerships with Blackstone and Goldman Sachs, indicating institutional validation of AI's threat to traditional analyst workflows

- **Template architecture democratizes AI deployment** - Each agent combines three components: skills (domain knowledge in markdown files), connectors (data access), and subagents (specialized Claude models), making sophisticated automation accessible to non-technical users

- **Research automation targets core analyst tasks** - Five templates directly challenge human analysts: pitch builders that create target lists and comparables, meeting preparers that brief clients, earnings reviewers that update models, model builders that maintain financials, and market researchers that synthesize news and broker research

- **Open source strategy accelerates adoption** - All templates are freely available on GitHub with simple installation commands for Claude Cowork and Claude Code, removing traditional barriers to enterprise AI implementation

- **Skills files reveal simplicity beneath complexity** - The meeting prep agent runs on just 400 words of text instructions plus a 220-word agent file, suggesting that effective financial AI requires domain knowledge structure rather than complex coding

- **Installation friction remains low** - Users can deploy agents through simple URL pasting in Cowork or single command lines in Claude Code, with background operation and slash command activation (like /comps, /dcf, /earnings)

- **Activation gap creates competitive opportunity** - While templates can onboard newcomers quickly, they lack deep domain expertise codification, creating advantages for firms that can properly customize these tools with proprietary knowledge
