Cerebras achieved a $63 billion IPO valuation by betting on dinner plate-sized AI chips that deliver 20x faster inference than standard GPUs, culminating in a $20 billion deal with OpenAI that materialized in just four weeks. The company's wafer-scale computing approach required years of patient capital and engineering persistence while being dangerously ahead of market demand.

---

- **Wafer-scale engineering breakthrough** - Cerebras built chips the size of dinner plates using entire silicon wafers, delivering 20x faster AI inference speeds compared to traditional postage stamp-sized chips from Nvidia and AMD

- **Market timing survival** - The company endured a "brutal period" of being years ahead of AI demand, requiring exceptional persistence and investor patience before the market caught up to their technology

- **Rapid OpenAI partnership** - A remarkable $20 billion deal with OpenAI came together in only four weeks, demonstrating how breakthrough performance can accelerate enterprise sales cycles

- **Speed unlocks new business models** - The extreme performance advantages enable entirely new AI applications and use cases that weren't economically viable with slower traditional GPU infrastructure

- **"Professional David" strategy** - Feldman advocates for the mindset of being a persistent underdog challenger against tech giants, requiring fortitude to compete with much larger, well-resourced competitors

- **Post-training workload focus** - Cerebras is positioning for the growing market of AI inference and post-training workloads rather than just initial model training, where speed becomes even more critical

- **IPO timing rationale** - Going public provides access to capital markets and credibility needed to compete with established chip giants in the rapidly expanding AI infrastructure market
