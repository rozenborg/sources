Rahul Vohra, Superhuman's CEO, reveals that most founders fundamentally misunderstand product-market fit by focusing on growth metrics rather than user satisfaction depth, and argues that premium positioning can paradoxically limit market expansion unless paired with extreme constraint and focus on power users who desperately need the solution.

---

- **Product-market fit misconception** Most founders chase vanity metrics like user growth instead of measuring how disappointed users would be if the product disappeared, leading to premature scaling
- **Premium positioning trap** High-end pricing can actually hurt businesses by creating unrealistic expectations and limiting addressable market size unless the value proposition is genuinely transformative
- **Deliberate constraint strategy** Superhuman succeeded by intentionally limiting features and focusing solely on email power users rather than trying to build a universal communication platform
- **LinkedIn acquisition insights** Vohra's previous company Reportive was acquired not for its technology but for its user base and team, highlighting the importance of building valuable user relationships over features
- **Gaming industry origins** Experience designing RuneScape taught Vohra about user engagement loops and retention mechanics that directly translated to building sticky productivity software
- **Silicon Valley adoption pattern** Superhuman's success came from targeting specific user personas (VCs, executives, power emailers) who could afford premium pricing and had genuine pain with existing solutions
- **Satisfaction-first metrics** The company's breakthrough came from measuring user disappointment scores rather than traditional engagement metrics, revealing true product-market fit
