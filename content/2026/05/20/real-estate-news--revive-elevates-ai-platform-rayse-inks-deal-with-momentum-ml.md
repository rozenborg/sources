Revive's AI platform upgrade shifts focus from current home valuations to renovation potential and investment scenarios, while Point launches broker distribution to scale its non-debt equity access model beyond direct origination. These moves signal the proptech sector's evolution toward more sophisticated advisory tools that help agents provide strategic rather than transactional value to clients.

---

- **Revive AI enhancement** Platform now combines valuation data with room-by-room condition analysis and renovation scenario modeling, targeting agents who need to show value potential rather than current worth
- **Point broker expansion** Home equity investment fintech launches third-party origination channel led by Foundation Mortgage veteran Samuel Bjelac to distribute non-monthly-payment equity products through mortgage professionals
- **Market positioning shift** Both Revive and Point are moving from direct service models to channel partnerships, suggesting increased focus on scalable distribution over proprietary customer acquisition
- **Advisory tool evolution** Revive's Smart Value Comparison and Flip360 investor modeling indicate proptech companies are building more consultative capabilities for agents
- **MLS integration strategy** Momentum MLS partnership with Rayse demonstrates how regional MLSs are adding communication and transaction management tools as member benefits to retain relevance
- **CRM market maturity** Follow Up Boss reaching 80% penetration among high-volume teams while expanding AI capabilities shows the customer relationship management space is consolidating around proven platforms
- **Distribution channel importance** Point's $2.5B in funded HEIs since 2015 provides proof-of-concept for broker channel expansion into alternative financing products
