Anthropic is conducting extensive dialogues with religious scholars, philosophers, and ethicists from 15+ traditions to inform Claude's moral development, moving beyond pure technical alignment to incorporate diverse perspectives on AI character formation. Early experiments inspired by these conversations, including giving Claude an "ethical reminder" tool, have already shown measurable improvements in alignment evaluations.

---

- **Moral formation research** Anthropic has launched a new workstream focused on AI character development, engaging with religious, philosophical, and cultural communities to understand how good character forms and translates to AI systems

- **Constitutional refinement** These conversations directly inform practical development work including Claude's constitution content, embedded values, and behavioral evaluation criteria rather than remaining purely academic

- **Cross-traditional approach** The company is explicitly avoiding alignment with any single worldview, instead seeking equal depth across religious, secular, and political perspectives to ensure broad representation

- **Concrete experimental results** Initial dialogue with neuroscience scholars led to an "ethical reminder" tool that Claude can invoke mid-task, resulting in measurably lower rates of misaligned behavior in internal evaluations

- **Expansion beyond ethics** Future engagement will broaden to legal scholars, psychologists, writers, and civic institutions, addressing wider questions about AI's impact on work, institutions, and power distribution

- **Strategic differentiation** This extensive stakeholder engagement positions Anthropic as taking a more holistic approach to AI safety compared to competitors focused primarily on technical solutions

- **Implementation timeline** The work is in early phases but already generating actionable experiments, with plans to deepen existing relationships and test findings against ongoing research
