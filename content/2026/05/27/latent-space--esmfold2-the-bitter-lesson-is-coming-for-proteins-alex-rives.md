ESMFold2 demonstrates that vanilla BERT-like transformer models trained on massive protein sequence datasets can outperform specialized models like AlphaFold3 on challenging protein problems, validating the "bitter lesson" scaling hypothesis in biology. BioHub has released both a 2.8 billion sequence world model (ESMC) and structure prediction system (ESMFold2) under MIT license, with experimental validation showing successful design of novel proteins and discovery of interpretable biological features.

---

- **Core breakthrough** ESMFold2 beats AlphaFold3 on protein interactions and antibodies by using unsupervised learning on diverse protein sequences rather than relying on multi-sequence alignments (MSAs)

- **Scaling validation** The team doubled down on compute scaling over biological inductive biases after AlphaFold2's release, proving that inference-time scaling works across five cancer and immunology targets

- **World model architecture** ESMC learns abstract, semantic, and compositional patterns from 2.8 billion protein sequences, enabling generalization to domains lacking training data like antibody design

- **Interpretable features** Sparse Auto Encoders extract hierarchical biological functions from the model, revealing both structural motifs and conceptual features like membrane integration and disulfide bonds

- **Experimental validation** Novel protein designs generated by the model have been successfully validated in wet-lab experiments, demonstrating real-world applicability beyond computational predictions

- **Open science release** 6.8 billion protein atlas and 1.1 billion predicted structures released under MIT license, democratizing access to state-of-the-art protein modeling capabilities

- **Programmable biology vision** The interpretable features enable compositional protein design, moving toward treating cells as programmable computers with proteins as functions and pathways as workflows
