CFOs are discovering that traditional financial expertise alone no longer suffices as AI transforms finance roles, with leaders now prioritizing digital skills, analytics capabilities, and soft skills like creativity and empathy over conventional technical proficiency. Despite this recognition, most organizations have failed to meaningfully invest in upskilling their teams, while workers increasingly value job flexibility and recognition over compensation and career advancement.

---

- **Skills Gap Mismatch** AI hasn't eliminated transactional work as expected from 2022 predictions, with workers still handling routine processing while CFOs struggle to find talent with cash flow management, financial analysis, and generative AI experience

- **Hiring Strategy Shift** Finance leaders are moving beyond traditional financial metrics to recruit for digital, data science, and analytics capabilities, plus soft skills like teaming, creativity, and empathy that don't appear on résumés

- **Training Over Hiring** Organizations prefer upskilling existing workforce over external recruitment for AI-related technical skills, though relatively few have made meaningful investments in these development programs

- **Employee Value Evolution** Workers now prioritize job flexibility and work recognition over compensation and career growth, creating a "stagility" dynamic that balances team agility with individual stability

- **Technology Integration Challenges** Half of surveyed organizations report employee engagement issues, with nearly as many facing resistance to new technology and skilled talent shortages at the intersection of human capability and AI implementation

- **Strategic Focus Transition** Finance teams are evolving from data cleansing and AI training toward data monetization and value creation over the next five years, requiring leaders to build centers of excellence for routine task automation

- **Workforce Expectations Gap** Meaningful work has become less important to employees but more critical to leaders, while workers seek digital upskilling and AI integration as their top desired work experiences
