Manufacturing companies face a critical gap between executive AI enthusiasm and worker skepticism that threatens successful implementation. A cross-industry study reveals that over 75% of frontline workers are dissatisfied with AI training programs, creating widespread distrust that could undermine the $13 trillion productivity gains executives expect from automation.

---

- **Worker-Management Perception Gap** Three-quarters of 85 frontline workers across six industries expressed dissatisfaction with AI training, contrasting sharply with executive optimism about transformation potential

- **Trust Deficit** Workers demonstrate deep skepticism toward both AI tools themselves and the organizations deploying them, indicating fundamental communication and engagement failures

- **Training Inadequacy** Current training programs are failing to prepare workers for AI integration, with most participants reporting insufficient preparation for technological changes in their roles

- **Job Security Uncertainty** Many workers lack clarity about how AI will reshape their specific roles and whether they will remain relevant in future manufacturing operations

- **Multi-Region Consistency** The skepticism spans workers in Australia, UK, and United States, suggesting systemic rather than localized implementation challenges

- **Strategic Risk** The disconnect between executive expectations and worker readiness creates significant implementation risk for AI initiatives across manufacturing sectors

- **Collaborative Imperative** Successful AI deployment requires building with workers as partners rather than implementing technology for passive adoption by reluctant users
