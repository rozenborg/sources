Baseten's 30x growth demonstrates that AI inference demand is exploding faster than supply can scale, with companies increasingly needing custom model serving rather than generic API calls. The inference infrastructure market is becoming the critical battleground where proprietary workflows and specialized post-training create sustainable competitive advantages.

---

- **Supply constraints driving consolidation** - GPU capacity shortages are forcing longer-term contracts (6+ months vs. previous spot pricing) and multi-cloud strategies across 18+ providers to meet demand
- **Custom models dominating workloads** - Generic ChatGPT-style applications losing ground to specialized inference serving fine-tuned models for specific enterprise workflows like medical transcription and support automation
- **Post-training as competitive moat** - Companies with unique user signals can create defensible value by encoding proprietary data into specialized models rather than relying on frontier model APIs
- **Application layer persistence** - Despite fears of commoditization, the application layer remains valuable when companies build custom inference pipelines that capture domain-specific user behavior and feedback
- **Infrastructure software layer key** - Raw compute becoming commodity; differentiation happening in orchestration, optimization, and operational tooling that reduces latency and improves reliability
- **Chinese models entering US market** - Geopolitical considerations around model provenance becoming important as Chinese AI models gain technical competitiveness but face regulatory scrutiny
- **Operational complexity at scale** - Managing inference across 90+ clusters requires sophisticated monitoring, with edge cases and reliability issues becoming primary engineering challenges
- **Efficiency paradox driving growth** - Better inference optimization reduces per-request costs but increases overall demand, creating net expansion in total market size
