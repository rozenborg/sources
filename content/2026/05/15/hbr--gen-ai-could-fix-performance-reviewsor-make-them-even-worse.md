Generative AI is transforming corporate performance reviews by dramatically reducing the time managers spend writing evaluations, with early adopters like Citi, JPMorgan, and BCG reporting significant efficiency gains, but organizations risk amplifying existing biases and superficiality if they simply automate traditional review formats rather than fundamentally rethinking performance evaluation.

---

- **Efficiency breakthrough** Major financial services and consulting firms are achieving 40%+ time savings on performance review writing through AI automation
- **Data integration advantage** Systems like Citi's Performance Assist aggregate organization-wide data to create more comprehensive employee evaluations than manual processes
- **Automation trap risk** Most implementations focus on polishing traditional narrative reviews rather than addressing fundamental flaws in performance evaluation methodology
- **Bias amplification concern** AI systems may systematically encode and scale existing human biases present in historical performance data and evaluation patterns
- **Quality vs. quantity tension** Faster, more polished reviews don't necessarily translate to better feedback, coaching, or employee development outcomes
- **Strategic implementation gap** Organizations need to redesign their performance philosophy before deploying AI, not just digitize existing broken processes
