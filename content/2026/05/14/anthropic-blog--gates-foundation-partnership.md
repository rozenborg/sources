Anthropic commits $200 million over four years with the Gates Foundation to deploy Claude AI for global health, education, and economic mobility programs, targeting 4.6 billion people lacking essential health services and creating public AI tools for vaccine development, disease modeling, and agricultural productivity. This partnership represents Anthropic's largest beneficial deployment initiative, positioning Claude as infrastructure for addressing market failures in critical social sectors.

---

- **Global health focus** targeting 4.6 billion people without essential health services through AI-powered vaccine screening, disease outbreak detection, and health ministry decision support systems
- **Drug discovery acceleration** using Claude to computationally screen vaccine candidates for polio and therapies for HPV (350K annual deaths) and preeclampsia before costly pre-clinical trials
- **Disease modeling integration** with Institute for Disease Modeling to make malaria and tuberculosis treatment deployment forecasts accessible to non-specialist practitioners
- **Public goods creation** including agriculture datasets, educational benchmarks, and health evaluation frameworks released as open resources for broader AI development
- **Educational AI tools** spanning K-12 tutoring in the US to foundational literacy apps in sub-Saharan Africa and India through the Global AI for Learning Alliance
- **Economic mobility infrastructure** building portable skill certification systems and linking training program data to employment outcomes for measuring intervention effectiveness
- **Strategic positioning** as Anthropic's answer to market failures in social impact, competing with OpenAI's humanitarian initiatives while building Claude's reputation in mission-critical applications
- **Beneficial Deployments expansion** signals major investment in non-commercial applications, potentially creating new revenue models through discounted nonprofit access and technical support services
