Consumer trust in AI for homebuying has plummeted from 30% to 16% in just one year, even as three-quarters of buyers expect the technology to be involved in their transactions. While younger buyers see AI as potentially beneficial for speed and efficiency, the industry faces a critical trust deficit that's driving demand for human oversight and mandatory disclosure requirements.

---

- **Trust Collapse** Consumer confidence in AI for home search dropped by nearly half year-over-year, with 70% across all demographics considering AI errors unacceptable during the "learning phase"

- **Generational Divide** Millennials and Gen Z view AI as an asset and expect it to accelerate processes, with half of Gen Z saying AI involvement would boost their buying confidence, yet they're paradoxically more willing to pay for human verification than older buyers

- **Transparency Imperative** 68% of buyers demand clear AI-use disclosure in listings and mortgage recommendations, with 61% of baby boomers wanting mandatory labeling requirements

- **Human Preference Surge** US buyers increasingly favor human interaction, with 55% preferring people over AI for mortgage processes (up from 46%) and two-thirds wanting human legal assistance (up from 54%)

- **Revenue Opportunity** 44% of buyers will pay for human verification of AI decisions, creating a potential service differentiator for real estate professionals who can bridge the AI-human gap

- **Lending Efficiency Potential** AI could reduce loan processing by 1-3 months, particularly valuable since 37% of US loans go to under-35 borrowers who expect faster digital processes

- **Data Integrity Concerns** 64% worry about AI "recycling" unverified information and 46% believe lenders shouldn't conduct AI valuations without explicit approval, highlighting need for robust data governance
