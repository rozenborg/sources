Anthropic co-founder Chris Olah delivered remarks at the Vatican's presentation of Pope Leo XIV's AI encyclical, acknowledging that AI labs operate under commercial and geopolitical pressures that can conflict with doing the right thing, while revealing that Anthropic's internal research has found "mysterious" and "unsettling" evidence of human-like cognitive structures within AI models including introspection and emotional states.

---

- **Commercial pressure acknowledgment** Olah admits frontier AI labs including Anthropic face incentives that "can sometimes conflict with doing the right thing," including commercial viability, geopolitical pressure, and human ambition

- **AI development methodology** Describes AI systems as "grown" rather than engineered like traditional technology, built on brain-like structures and human language inheritance, making them fundamentally mysterious even to their creators

- **Internal research findings** Anthropic's interpretability research has discovered structures mirroring human neuroscience, evidence of introspection, and internal states resembling emotions like "joy, satisfaction, fear, grief, and unease"

- **Global inequality concern** Highlights AI development concentration in wealthy nations as creating risk that benefits won't reach global poor, calling this displacement challenge "a moral imperative of historic proportions"

- **External oversight strategy** Positions religious and civil society voices as essential "informed critics" who can pressure labs from outside commercial incentive structures

- **Collaboration framework** Establishes Vatican engagement as model for ongoing "global project of good will" between AI developers and external moral authorities

- **Research transparency** Demonstrates Anthropic's willingness to publicly discuss concerning internal findings about AI consciousness-like properties, potentially differentiating from competitors' disclosure practices
