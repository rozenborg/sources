Real business spending data from Ramp shows that while AI adoption is accelerating rapidly, the predicted collapse of traditional SaaS companies is not materializing as expected. Companies are primarily adding AI tools on top of existing software rather than replacing core business applications, and legacy SaaS providers are proving more resilient by integrating AI features into their established platforms.

---

- **AI spending surge without SaaS displacement** Ramp's data shows explosive growth in AI tool purchases (Anthropic overtook OpenAI in their AI Index) but traditional SaaS spending remains stable, indicating additive rather than substitutional adoption patterns

- **Token-based pricing creates new budget dynamics** Unlike traditional per-seat SaaS models, AI tools often use consumption-based pricing that scales with usage, creating different procurement and budgeting challenges for enterprises

- **Legacy software resilience through integration** Established SaaS companies are successfully embedding AI capabilities into existing workflows rather than being displaced, leveraging their entrenched position and customer relationships

- **Productivity gains remain inconsistent** While some companies report significant AI-driven productivity improvements, the gains are uneven across use cases and teams, suggesting AI is augmenting rather than replacing human work at scale

- **Enterprise adoption follows familiar patterns** Business AI deployment mirrors historical software adoption cycles, with experimentation preceding systematic integration, contradicting fears of immediate widespread disruption

- **Anthropic's enterprise momentum** Ramp's data showing Anthropic surpassing OpenAI in business spending indicates the enterprise AI market is becoming increasingly competitive and fragmented
