Enterprise AI initiatives are stalling at the critical "last mile" phase where promising pilots must scale into production systems, creating a dangerous gap between C-suite expectations and operational reality that threatens to undermine billions in AI investments.

---

- **Pilot proliferation without production** Most large companies have launched hundreds of AI pilots but struggle to transition successful experiments into enterprise-scale deployments that deliver measurable business value

- **Infrastructure readiness gap** Organizations lack the data governance, integration capabilities, and operational frameworks needed to support AI applications beyond sandbox environments

- **Skills and change management bottleneck** Technical teams can build AI prototypes but lack expertise in production MLOps, while business units resist workflow changes required for AI adoption

- **ROI measurement challenges** Companies cannot demonstrate clear returns on AI investments because pilots remain isolated from core business processes where impact can be quantified

- **Executive expectation misalignment** C-suite leaders expect rapid transformation based on pilot success stories, creating pressure that may lead to premature scaling or abandoned initiatives

- **Competitive risk amplification** Organizations that solve the last-mile problem first will gain significant operational advantages while competitors remain stuck in perpetual pilot mode

- **Strategic imperative for systematic approach** Companies must shift from ad-hoc experimentation to structured AI transformation programs with clear production pathways and success metrics
