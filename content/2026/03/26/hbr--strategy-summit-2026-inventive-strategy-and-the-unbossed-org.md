Columbia Business School's Rita McGrath argues that the economy's shift from physical assets to intangible goods demands a fundamental rethinking of strategy, with companies needing to choose a clear "center" around which to build capacity rather than relying on traditional competitive advantages. Her research on Novartis shows how centering around "inventive medicines" and adopting "unbossed" organizational structures unlocked $380 billion in combined value after strategic divestitures.

---

- **Strategic Centering is Critical** - Companies must choose their center of gravity before making any other strategic moves, as this resolves capital allocation dilemmas and creates bounded opportunity sets that clarify what to pursue and what to avoid

- **Unbossed Structure Drives Innovation** - Permissionless organizations with "curious, inspired, unbossed" cultures enable smart people to take initiative without hierarchical approval, as demonstrated by Novartis's breakthrough Super Bowl breast cancer awareness campaign

- **Decision Architecture Matters** - Leaders should categorize decisions into three types: obvious (push to edges), complicated (assemble expertise), and complex (require leadership judgment), while avoiding the momentum-killing default of indecision

- **Small Teams With Clear Mission** - Loosely coupled teams of 3-12 people with tight mission alignment outperform large hierarchical structures, following the "two pizza rule" to minimize coordination overhead

- **Simplification Before Innovation** - Apply "SDSS times two": Stop Doing Stupid Stuff to eliminate organizational drag from outdated procedures, then Start Doing Smart Stuff to enable breakthrough thinking

- **Edge-Based Experimentation** - Create mechanisms like Adobe's Kickbox program ($1,000 gift cards for any employee experiment) to capture innovation from employees closest to customers and operations

- **Intangible Economy Demands New Metrics** - With company valuations now primarily based on patents, software, and technology rather than physical assets, traditional strategy frameworks centered on physical barriers are becoming obsolete
