Zillow's new AI mode will handle basic research questions that previously consumed agent time, potentially creating more informed clients but also reducing touchpoints between consumers and agents during the critical early stages of home shopping. The conversational AI tool remembers user preferences and can answer complex queries like rent-versus-buy decisions, challenging agents to differentiate through expertise and local market knowledge.

---

- **Core functionality** Zillow AI mode uses conversational interface instead of traditional filters, remembering user preferences across sessions and answering complex questions like "Is it better to rent or buy in Miami?" using Zillow's listing and pricing data

- **Agent displacement risk** The AI handles research-heavy questions that agents typically answered early in client relationships, potentially reducing initial client contact opportunities and shifting agents toward later-stage transaction work

- **Client quality improvement** Zillow expects the tool to produce "more informed, higher-intent clients" by pre-educating them on affordability and market basics before they contact agents, potentially improving conversion rates

- **Competitive differentiation pressure** As AI handles standard market information, agents will need to focus more heavily on expertise, judgment, and hyperlocal knowledge to maintain value proposition with clients

- **Popular query types** Early testing shows users primarily ask about affordability, neighborhoods, and home feature comparisons, with Zillow expecting more transaction-focused questions as adoption grows

- **Market timing advantage** Launch comes less than six months after Zillow's ChatGPT integration, positioning the platform ahead of competitors in AI-driven property search during industry-wide AI adoption wave

- **Trust and compliance safeguards** Zillow has implemented guardrails to prevent discriminatory steering and balances information provision with service promotion to maintain user trust while monetizing the platform
