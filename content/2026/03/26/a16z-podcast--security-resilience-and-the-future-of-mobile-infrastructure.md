The Navy is revolutionizing its tech acquisition by running bootcamps for program managers and piloting commercial solutions in months rather than years, while the Salt Typhoon breach exposing Chinese infiltration of U.S. cellular networks has created urgent demand for secure mobile alternatives that companies like Cape are racing to provide.

---

- **Navy acquisition transformation** The Navy now runs bootcamps teaching program managers to work with commercial tech companies and has streamlined processes to pilot solutions in 3-6 months instead of traditional multi-year timelines

- **Salt Typhoon breach implications** Chinese hackers infiltrated major U.S. cellular carriers for months or years, accessing call records, text messages, and potentially real-time communications of senior government officials and political figures

- **Cellular network vulnerability** Traditional telecom infrastructure has fundamental security weaknesses that cannot be easily patched, creating systemic national security risks that require architectural alternatives

- **Cape's secure mobile solution** The company has developed cellular infrastructure that provides end-to-end encryption and can be deployed rapidly for sensitive government and military communications

- **Defense tech sales strategy** Successful government contractors must understand that military buyers prioritize mission success over cost optimization, requiring different product positioning than commercial markets

- **Rapid deployment capability** Cape can establish secure cellular networks in days rather than months, crucial for military operations and emergency response scenarios

- **Infrastructure resilience gap** Current cellular networks lack the redundancy and security features needed for critical national security communications, representing both a vulnerability and a market opportunity
