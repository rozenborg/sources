Agentic AI security has emerged as the dominant conversation at RSAC 2026, driven by the shadow proliferation of OpenClaw and its ecosystem of innovation. The market has responded with 30 standout security tools spanning seven critical categories, signaling that agent-based threats and protections have moved from niche research to mainstream enterprise concern.

---

- **Agent Discovery & Inventory** dominates with 6 tools including Palo Alto's Prisma AIRS 3.0 and CrowdStrike Falcon, addressing the core challenge of tracking dynamic agents across cloud, SaaS, and endpoints that traditional scanners miss

- **Identity & Authorization** features 4 specialized solutions like PlainID and Aembit moving beyond static API keys to context-aware policies and workload identity for non-human identities (NHIs)

- **Runtime Protection** spans 5 vendors including Cisco and Wiz, focusing on governance and real-time monitoring of agent behavior during execution

- **Critical Security Gap** identified in agent-to-agent (A2A) communication monitoring, with only Reva.ai showing promising intent-based access control solutions

- **Shadow AI Proliferation** creates inventory blindspots as business units deploy agents through low-code platforms like Microsoft Copilot Studio, driving demand for Agent Security Posture Management (ASPM)

- **MCP Gateway Controls** emerge as key chokepoint for enforcement, with 5 tools from Netskope, SentinelOne, and others providing policy enforcement before tool execution

- **Enterprise Readiness** varies significantly by use case, with established vendors like Palo Alto and CrowdStrike offering comprehensive platforms while startups focus on specialized capabilities like behavioral telemetry and agent architecture parsing
