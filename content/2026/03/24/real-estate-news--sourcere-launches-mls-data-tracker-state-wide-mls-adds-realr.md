SourceRE's new Data Dye system gives MLSs the ability to track their listing data through invisible identifiers that follow information as it moves through vendor feeds, potentially ending years of suspected unauthorized data usage in gray-market channels. Meanwhile, major platforms are doubling down on AI integration, with Offerpad reporting 200 basis point increases in contracting rates from its new machine learning systems and State-Wide MLS adopting comprehensive AI-powered property tools for its 6,000+ subscribers.

---

- **Data tracking breakthrough** SourceRE's Data Dye embeds invisible identifiers in MLS listing data, creating verifiable trails when data appears in unauthorized channels and providing centralized violation monitoring
- **AI performance gains** Offerpad's SCOUT platform uses machine learning to improve lead quality, achieving 200 basis point increases in contracting rates while streamlining seller acquisition workflows  
- **MLS modernization** State-Wide MLS's partnership with RealReports brings AI property advisor "Aiden" and consolidated data from 100 sources to 6,000+ subscribers, replacing existing tax record providers
- **FSBO expansion** Ownli's 43-state launch now covers 500,000+ listings worth $313 billion, with users reportedly saving average $42,832 in commissions compared to traditional agent structures
- **Insurance disruption** Two new AI-powered insurance platforms launch targeting property-specific risk assessment (ZestyAI's Z-SPARK) and California market deal protection (Rhino's DealShield)
- **Market implications** Technology adoption is accelerating across MLS infrastructure, iBuying operations, and insurance sectors as companies seek data-driven competitive advantages
- **Revenue protection** Data tracking tools like Data Dye could significantly impact unauthorized listing aggregation businesses that rely on gray-market data sources
