The emergence of artificial general intelligence will fundamentally restructure labor markets and wealth distribution, creating unprecedented opportunities for human-AI collaboration while potentially concentrating economic gains among those who control AGI systems and complementary assets.

---

- **Labor market transformation** - AGI will automate cognitive tasks across professions, but human work will shift toward areas requiring emotional intelligence, creativity, and tasks that benefit from human-AI collaboration rather than pure substitution

- **Wealth concentration risk** - Economic benefits may accrue primarily to AGI system owners and those controlling complementary assets like data, infrastructure, and distribution networks, potentially exacerbating inequality

- **New value creation models** - AGI could enable entirely new categories of products and services that were previously impossible, creating markets and opportunities that don't exist today

- **Skills premium shift** - The premium will move toward uniquely human capabilities and the ability to effectively collaborate with AGI systems, requiring significant workforce retraining and adaptation

- **Policy intervention necessity** - Governments may need to consider redistribution mechanisms or universal basic income approaches to address potential mass displacement and ensure broader participation in AGI-generated wealth

- **Timing uncertainty creates planning challenges** - The unpredictable timeline for AGI deployment makes it difficult for individuals, companies, and policymakers to prepare adequately for the transition
