OWASP has released version 0.8 of its Agentic AI Vulnerability Scoring System (AIVSS), marking the first standardized framework for quantifying security risks in autonomous AI systems, with strategic backing from AI insurance underwriters through AIUC-1 co-publication. This release incorporates 1,900+ industry comments and establishes critical mappings to major security frameworks, positioning it as the emerging standard for enterprise AI agent risk assessment ahead of the anticipated v1.0 release by end of 2026.

---

- **Quantitative Risk Model** Enhanced mathematical framework specifically designed to calculate "agentic risk amplification" factors like autonomy, tool use, and persistent memory that traditional vulnerability scoring systems cannot capture

- **Insurance Industry Integration** Strategic co-publication with AIUC-1 creates direct pathway for cyber insurance underwriting of AI agent deployments, potentially enabling risk transfer and compliance validation in enterprise environments

- **Framework Interoperability** New mappings to OWASP GenAI/LLM Top 10, CSA MAESTRO threat modeling, and NIST AI RMF provide comprehensive integration with existing security workflows and regulatory requirements

- **Empirical Validation** Addition of expert survey data in Appendix D provides evidence-based risk rankings, moving beyond theoretical frameworks to data-driven vulnerability prioritization

- **Dual Assessment Approach** Parallel launch of SSVC-based qualitative decision tree complements quantitative scoring, offering both numerical risk scores and actionable remediation prioritization for security teams

- **Community-Driven Development** Public review period opening April 16, 2026 for v1.0 input, with active solicitation of feedback from 1,900+ previous contributors across enterprise and open-source communities

- **Market Timing** Release ahead of RSA Conference 2026 positions AIVSS as the de facto standard during peak industry adoption of AI agents, with leadership presenting at main conference track March 23rd
