Great founders fail not from lack of vision but from decision paralysis and over-deference to senior hires, losing the conviction that got them started. The most successful founder-CEOs master "constructive confrontation" - directly addressing problems instead of letting decision debt accumulate and destroy their companies.

---

- **Decision debt crisis** Founders accumulate "decision debt" by avoiding hard choices, leading to organizational paralysis worse than technical debt
- **Over-deference trap** First-time founders lose confidence and defer too much to experienced senior hires, abandoning the judgment that made them successful initially  
- **VP of Sales mistake** This is the role founders consistently mess up most - either hiring too early, choosing wrong profiles, or failing to manage the function properly
- **Constructive confrontation** Andy Grove's approach of directly addressing problems and conflicts is what separates great founder-CEOs from those who fail
- **Founder mode boundaries** The concept works for product and vision decisions but breaks down when applied universally to all company functions
- **Culture through behavior** Zuckerberg, Huang, and Musk succeed because they define culture through consistent actions and decisions, not stated values or mission statements
- **Confidence preservation** Great founders maintain their original conviction while learning to channel it through proper organizational structures as they scale
