Anthropic is challenging a Department of War supply chain risk designation in court while emphasizing the narrow scope affects only direct DoD contract usage of Claude, not the majority of customers. CEO Dario Amodei apologized for leaked internal communications and committed to continuing support for military operations during any transition period.

---

- **Legal Challenge**: Anthropic will contest the DoD supply chain risk designation in court, arguing the action lacks legal soundness while noting the narrow statutory scope under 10 USC 3252

- **Limited Customer Impact**: The designation only affects Claude usage directly tied to Department of War contracts, not broader commercial relationships or unrelated uses by DoD contractors

- **Operational Continuity**: Company pledges to provide models at nominal cost with engineering support to ensure warfighters retain AI capabilities during ongoing combat operations and transition periods

- **Policy Red Lines**: Anthropic maintains two exceptions preventing involvement in fully autonomous weapons and mass domestic surveillance while supporting intelligence analysis, cyber operations, and operational planning

- **Internal Communications Crisis**: CEO apologized for leaked internal post written during a chaotic day following presidential announcements and competing AI partnerships, acknowledging the tone didn't reflect considered company positions

- **Strategic Positioning**: Despite legal dispute, Anthropic emphasizes shared commitment with DoD on national security priorities and AI deployment across government agencies

- **Competitive Dynamics**: The controversy emerged amid announcements of Pentagon partnerships with OpenAI, highlighting intensifying competition for government AI contracts among major providers
