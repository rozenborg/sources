Atlassian's CEO argues the current SaaS selloff is creating artificial panic, as AI will eliminate some software categories entirely while strengthening others that focus on human collaboration and complex workflows rather than simple record-keeping. The real challenge isn't AI replacing software, but designing AI agents that everyday enterprise users will actually trust and adopt in their daily work.

---

- **SaaS market dynamics** - Current selloff reflects investor uncertainty rather than fundamental weakness across all software categories, creating opportunities for discerning companies
- **AI disruption thesis** - Software built around "systems of record" (databases, CRM) faces existential threat from AI, while "systems of process" (collaboration, workflow) remain defensible
- **Atlassian positioning** - Company views its collaboration-focused products as AI-resistant because they facilitate human interaction and complex decision-making that AI cannot fully replace
- **Enterprise AI adoption** - Main barrier is user trust rather than technical capability, requiring careful UX design to gradually introduce AI capabilities without overwhelming users
- **Agent deployment strategy** - Success depends on starting with simple, transparent AI tasks before progressing to more autonomous agents that users can understand and control
- **Market consolidation** - AI disruption will likely accelerate winner-take-all dynamics in software, benefiting companies that successfully integrate AI while maintaining user trust
- **Workflow transformation** - Future competitive advantage lies in helping users reimagine processes rather than simply automating existing tasks
