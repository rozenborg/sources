Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.5 achieves state-of-the-art performance in software engineering while cutting token usage by up to 76% and reducing pricing to $5/$25 per million tokens, making frontier AI capabilities economically viable for widespread enterprise adoption. The model outperformed all human candidates on Anthropic's internal engineering assessment and introduces dynamic effort controls that let developers optimize for either speed or maximum capability.

---

- **Breakthrough Engineering Performance** Claude Opus 4.5 scored higher than any human candidate on Anthropic's notoriously difficult 2-hour performance engineering exam, signaling a potential inflection point in AI's impact on technical professions

- **Dramatic Efficiency Gains** The model uses 48-76% fewer tokens than predecessors while matching or exceeding performance, with customers reporting 50-75% reductions in coding errors and 65% fewer tokens for complex tasks

- **Strategic Pricing Move** At $5/$25 per million tokens, Anthropic has made Opus-level capabilities accessible to mainstream enterprise use cases, potentially disrupting the competitive landscape for high-end AI models

- **Advanced Agent Capabilities** New effort parameter allows dynamic control between speed and capability, while improved context management and multi-agent coordination enable 30-minute autonomous coding sessions and complex multi-system workflows

- **Enterprise Product Integration** Expanded availability across Chrome, Excel, desktop apps, and major cloud platforms with removed usage caps for Opus users, positioning Claude for deeper enterprise workflow penetration

- **Safety Leadership Claims** Anthropic asserts Opus 4.5 is the most robustly aligned frontier model available, with superior resistance to prompt injection attacks—a critical advantage for enterprise security requirements

- **Platform Ecosystem Expansion** Enhanced Developer Platform with composable building blocks, context compaction, and advanced tool use creates competitive moats while enabling more sophisticated AI applications
