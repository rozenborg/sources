AI compute scaling faces three critical bottlenecks that will fundamentally reshape the semiconductor industry by 2030, with ASML's advanced lithography equipment becoming the primary constraint as Nvidia's early TSMC allocation secures its dominance while squeezing competitors like Google out of cutting-edge nodes.

---

- **Logic bottleneck dominance** ASML's extreme ultraviolet (EUV) lithography machines will become the #1 constraint for AI compute scaling by 2030, limiting which companies can access the most advanced chip manufacturing nodes

- **Nvidia's strategic moat** Secured TSMC manufacturing allocation years in advance, creating a structural advantage that squeezes competitors like Google out of cutting-edge nodes and maintains pricing power despite technological maturity

- **Memory crunch incoming** A massive memory bottleneck is approaching that will constrain AI model scaling independently of logic improvements, requiring new architectural approaches and memory technologies

- **Power scaling solvable** Despite concerns, power infrastructure scaling in the US will not be the limiting factor for AI compute growth, with solutions available through grid improvements and data center optimization

- **China semiconductor timeline** China will not outscale the West in semiconductor manufacturing this decade due to equipment access restrictions, particularly around advanced lithography tools

- **Hedge fund arbitrage gap** Most hedge funds are not making the obvious AGI trade despite clear semiconductor supply chain constraints and pricing dynamics, representing a potential market inefficiency

- **TSMC allocation pressure** Apple may face pressure to give up some advanced node capacity to AI chip customers as demand for cutting-edge manufacturing outstrips supply

- **Space manufacturing timeline** Space-based GPU manufacturing and deployment will not happen this decade due to technical and economic constraints, despite theoretical advantages
