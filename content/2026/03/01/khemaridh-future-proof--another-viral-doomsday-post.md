## Summary

## Key Facts

- Market volatility has increased to point where single blog posts can trigger sell-offs
- "2028 Global Intelligence Crisis" scenario predicts short-term profit boom from layoffs followed by economic collapse
- Current period described as biggest career opportunity in lifetime for AI adoption
- Study shows 29% of workflows are deterministic and require zero AI/LLM integration
- High demand exists for "AI-native" employees but role definitions remain unclear
- Corporate AI upskilling programs are largely absent

## Strategic Implications

- Labor markets face dramatic transformation over next 3-4 years driven by AI adoption
- Window of opportunity exists where curiosity outweighs credentials in AI space
- Hybrid approach emerging: deterministic code for predictable tasks, LLMs for ambiguous work
- OpenAI potentially facing competitive pressure similar to Netscape's decline
- No coherent policy framework exists for managing AI's impact on employment

## Actionable Insights

- Prioritize personal AI upskilling over waiting for company training programs
- Focus on becoming "AI-native" through hands-on experimentation and risk-taking
- Develop skills in both deterministic automation and LLM integration
- Target roles that blend human judgment with AI capabilities
- Build practical AI applications rather than relying solely on chat interfaces
- Consider age/career stage advantage - earlier career professionals have structural opportunity
- Prepare for continued market volatility driven by AI speculation and adoption cycles
