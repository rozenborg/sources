Realtor.com has launched a ChatGPT integration to capture early-stage homebuyers through AI-powered conversations about affordability and neighborhoods, then funnel them back to its main platform for agent connections. This makes it the third major real estate portal to enter the AI search space, following Zillow and Redfin in a race to dominate the "pre-search" phase of homebuying.

---

- **Strategic positioning** Realtor.com is competing directly with Zillow and Redfin for early-stage buyer engagement, using ChatGPT as a top-of-funnel acquisition tool rather than a transaction replacement
- **Data protection measures** The company prohibits MLS data from training ChatGPT's model and only shows property previews within the chat interface, addressing industry concerns about licensing violations
- **Agent preservation strategy** The integration intentionally routes qualified leads back to Realtor.com's main platform where buyers connect with agents, positioning AI as a lead generation tool rather than agent replacement
- **Pre-search market capture** The app targets the 82% of Americans reportedly using AI for real estate insights, focusing on affordability calculations and neighborhood discovery before traditional property searches begin
- **Platform omnipresence goal** Realtor.com aims to be "omnipresent wherever consumers are having conversations about real estate," indicating broader AI integration plans beyond ChatGPT
- **MLS relationship leverage** The company emphasizes direct MLS partnerships over IDX feeds, potentially giving it competitive advantages in data access and compliance for AI applications
