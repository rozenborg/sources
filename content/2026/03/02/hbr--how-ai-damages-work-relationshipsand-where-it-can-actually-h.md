AI is creating a hidden crisis in workplace relationships by introducing cognitive burden, eroding trust, and eliminating the productive friction that builds authentic connections between colleagues. While promising efficiency, AI-mediated interactions are actually generating "workslop"—low-quality output that requires more effort to decode and undermines perceptions of competence and reliability among coworkers.

---

- **Cognitive overload** Workers spend extra mental energy questioning whether they're interacting with humans or AI, creating uncertainty that undermines relationship dynamics and forces colleagues to decode artificially generated content

- **Trust erosion** Research shows 42% view colleagues who send AI-generated work as less trustworthy, with half seeing them as less creative and capable, leading to damaged professional reputations and reluctance to collaborate

- **Productive friction loss** AI eliminates the "creative abrasion" and messy human interactions that generate breakthrough ideas, build understanding, and strengthen working relationships through shared problem-solving

- **Relationship building barriers** Authentic connections form through awkward moments and miscommunications, not smooth AI-mediated exchanges, potentially weakening the social bonds essential for effective teamwork

- **Transparency imperative** Organizations should mandate disclosure when AI is used in colleague interactions to reduce guesswork and cognitive burden while maintaining trust in professional relationships

- **Strategic application** Reserve AI for purely transactional relationships while using it to enhance rather than replace human connection through relationship-building prompts and meeting facilitation

- **Behavioral spillover risk** Transactional AI interaction patterns may reduce patience and civility with human colleagues, requiring explicit boundaries between AI and human communication norms
