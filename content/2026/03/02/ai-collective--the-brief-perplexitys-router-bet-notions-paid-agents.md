Perplexity is betting that coordinating multiple specialized AI models beats building one super-model, while Notion is turning workspace agents into a usage-based revenue stream that could either revolutionize team workflows or create unexpected budget headaches. Both moves signal that the real value in AI applications lies in orchestration layers rather than raw model ownership.

---

- **Router-first strategy**: Perplexity's Computer system routes different tasks to specialized models rather than forcing everything through one frontier model, prioritizing practical results over model allegiance

- **Orchestration advantage**: By not owning the underlying models, Perplexity can swap in any AI system when it performs best for specific tasks, avoiding vendor lock-in and performance bottlenecks  

- **Always-on automation**: Notion's Custom Agents run continuous workflows across connected tools like Slack and Calendar, moving beyond chat interfaces to actual autonomous work execution

- **Usage-based pricing risk**: Notion's credit system for agents creates potential budget surprises for enterprises, especially since useful agents tend to expand usage once embedded in workflows

- **Workflow lock-in**: Teams that wire their processes into Notion's agent layer face high switching costs, giving Notion significant retention leverage once adopted

- **Government AI tensions**: Trump's ban on federal use of Anthropic models after their refusal to drop surveillance safeguards creates a precedent that could reshape how AI companies negotiate with governments globally

- **Consumer momentum test**: Anthropic's surge to #1 on Apple's App Store following the federal ban highlights how regulatory conflicts can paradoxically boost consumer adoption
