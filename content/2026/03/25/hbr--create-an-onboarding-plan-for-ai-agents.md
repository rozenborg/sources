Organizations deploying AI agents are fundamentally misdiagnosing their implementation challenge, treating this as a technology adoption problem when it's actually a workforce management issue that requires systematic onboarding processes similar to those used for human employees.

---

- **Executive Misconception**: Leadership teams are approaching agentic AI as a technology integration challenge rather than recognizing it as a workforce expansion requiring human resources-style management

- **Work Management Priority**: The primary challenge lies in defining roles, responsibilities, and workflows for AI agents rather than mastering the underlying technology capabilities

- **Onboarding Framework Needed**: Organizations should develop structured onboarding processes for AI agents that mirror human employee integration, including role definition, training protocols, and performance metrics

- **Operational Integration**: Success depends on establishing clear boundaries and collaboration protocols between human workers and AI agents within existing business processes

- **Change Management Gap**: Current implementation failures stem from inadequate preparation of human teams to work alongside AI agents rather than technical limitations of the AI systems

- **Strategic Recommendation**: Treat AI agent deployment as organizational expansion requiring HR-style policies, not IT-style technology rollouts
