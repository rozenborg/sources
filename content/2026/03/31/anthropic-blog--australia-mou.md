Anthropic has signed a formal partnership with Australia's government, committing to share AI safety research and model evaluations while investing AUD$3 million in local research institutions focused on medical breakthroughs and rare disease diagnosis. The company is also establishing its first Asia-Pacific office in Sydney and launching a deep tech startup program, signaling a major expansion into the region.

---

- **Safety Institute Partnership** Anthropic will share model capabilities data and conduct joint evaluations with Australia's AI Safety Institute, mirroring existing arrangements with US, UK, and Japan safety institutes
- **Economic Intelligence Sharing** The company will provide Australian government access to Anthropic Economic Index data to track AI adoption across key sectors including natural resources, agriculture, healthcare, and financial services
- **Medical Research Investment** AUD$3 million in Claude API credits allocated to four institutions including Australian National University and Garvan Institute for genomic research, rare disease diagnosis, and pediatric medicine applications
- **Infrastructure Commitment** Anthropic is exploring data center and energy infrastructure investments throughout Australia, aligning with government expectations for local AI capabilities
- **Market Penetration Data** Australians already demonstrate the most diverse Claude usage patterns among English-speaking nations, indicating strong market readiness for expansion
- **Startup Ecosystem Support** New deep tech API credit program offers up to USD$50,000 for VC-backed startups in drug discovery, materials science, climate modeling, and medical diagnostics
- **Regional Expansion Signal** Sydney office opening represents Anthropic's first major Asia-Pacific presence, with local team announcements planned for coming weeks
