AI's economic transformation will unfold in three distinct stages requiring different policy responses, not the false either/or choices dominating current debates. Rather than choosing between retraining or UBI, between safety nets or economic restructuring, policymakers need integrated strategies that address near-term labor volatility, mid-term winner-take-all dynamics, and long-term questions about income distribution when human labor becomes largely obsolete.

---

- **Near-term volatility** focuses on managing AI-driven job displacement, particularly affecting entry-level workers who may lose traditional pathways to upward mobility, creating regional economic shocks similar to trade impacts on the American Midwest

- **Entrepreneurial opportunity window** emerges as AI enables one-person unicorns and allows startups to compress development timelines from years to months, though finding experienced talent to guide AI workflows becomes the critical bottleneck

- **Mid-term transition risks** include the rise of superstar firms with winner-take-all advantages from data and compute scale, plus fiscal system strain as payroll-based tax revenues decline while automation accelerates

- **Long-term structural challenge** centers on labor no longer being the primary path to income and economic security, breaking the foundational social contract of "work hard to get ahead" for growing population segments

- **Universal Basic Capital proposed** as superior to UBI because it grants permanent property rights rather than dependency on transfers, maintaining citizens as stakeholders rather than dependents in a democratic system

- **Strategic business implications** suggest labor arbitrage models will collapse as AI costs approach zero, while competitive advantages shift back toward large businesses with superior compute and data access

- **Policy integration framework** treats each intervention stage as prerequisite for the next, with near-term payment infrastructure eventually becoming delivery mechanisms for broader benefits reform
