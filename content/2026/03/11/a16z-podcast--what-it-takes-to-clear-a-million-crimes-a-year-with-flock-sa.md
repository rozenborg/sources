Flock Safety has scaled from solving neighborhood gun theft to operating license plate cameras and AI-powered crime-fighting tools across 6,000+ cities, clearing over one million crimes annually through real-time 911 integration and predictive policing technology. The company is expanding beyond cameras into drones and comprehensive city safety orchestration, positioning itself as critical infrastructure for law enforcement nationwide.

---

- **Origin story** Garrett Langley founded Flock Safety after a stolen gun incident in his Atlanta neighborhood, starting with license plate reader cameras to track criminal movement patterns
- **Massive scale deployment** The platform now operates in over 6,000 cities nationwide, demonstrating rapid adoption by law enforcement agencies seeking automated crime-solving tools
- **Million-crime milestone** Flock's technology contributed to clearing more than one million crimes in the past year, indicating significant impact on police clearance rates and investigative efficiency
- **Real-time integration** The system connects directly with 911 dispatch centers, enabling immediate alerts and response coordination when suspects or vehicles of interest are detected
- **AI orchestration layer** Beyond cameras, Flock is building comprehensive AI-powered tools that coordinate multiple data sources and safety technologies for city-wide crime prevention
- **Product expansion** The company is diversifying from license plate readers into drones and other surveillance technologies, creating an integrated ecosystem for law enforcement
- **Infrastructure positioning** Langley frames Flock as essential civic infrastructure rather than optional police technology, suggesting long-term stickiness and growth potential in the government market
