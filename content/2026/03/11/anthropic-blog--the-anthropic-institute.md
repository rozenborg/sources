Anthropic is launching The Anthropic Institute to study and publicly report on the societal challenges posed by rapidly advancing AI systems, predicting "far more dramatic progress" in the next two years that will bring extremely powerful AI much sooner than anticipated. Led by co-founder Jack Clark, the Institute will leverage Anthropic's unique position as a frontier AI developer to provide candid intelligence on AI capabilities and risks while expanding the company's policy influence through a new Washington D.C. office.

---

- **Accelerated AI timeline** Anthropic predicts "far more dramatic progress" in AI development over the next two years, citing compounding improvements and their CEO's vision of transformative AI arriving sooner than widely expected

- **Insider intelligence advantage** The Institute will have exclusive access to information "that only the builders of frontier AI systems possess," providing unique insights into cutting-edge AI capabilities and limitations

- **Three-team integration** Combines Anthropic's Frontier Red Team (capability stress-testing), Societal Impacts research, and Economic Research units under one umbrella to study AI's real-world effects comprehensively

- **High-profile academic recruits** Notable hires include Matt Botvinick (ex-Google DeepMind) for AI legal implications, Anton Korinek (University of Virginia economist) for economic transformation research, and Zoë Hitzig (ex-OpenAI) for economics-model development integration

- **Two-way engagement model** The Institute will both broadcast findings publicly and actively engage with displaced workers and affected communities to inform research priorities and company decisions

- **Policy expansion signal** Simultaneous launch of expanded Public Policy team with first D.C. office opening spring 2025 indicates Anthropic's growing focus on regulatory influence and government relations

- **Governance preparation** New teams focusing on AI progress forecasting and legal system interactions suggest preparation for potential regulatory frameworks around advanced AI systems
