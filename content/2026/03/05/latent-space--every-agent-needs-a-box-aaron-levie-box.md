Box CEO Aaron Levie argues that while coding agents have achieved remarkable success, the broader enterprise AI transformation will require years of workflow restructuring rather than simple drop-in automation. With Box serving 67% of the Fortune 500, Levie sees massive infrastructure opportunity in providing secure, governed data access for the inevitable explosion of enterprise agents that will outnumber human workers by orders of magnitude.

---

- **Coding agents succeeded due to perfect conditions** - Developers have broad codebase access, work in text-only medium, use tools they build themselves, and can easily share best practices, creating ideal conditions that don't exist elsewhere in enterprise workflows

- **Enterprise faces structural headwinds** - Unlike coding environments, enterprise workers have limited data access, work across multiple formats (calls, documents, conversations), lack comprehensive documentation practices, and operate under strict security controls

- **Identity and governance crisis looming** - Agents need distinct identities separate from human accounts since humans remain liable for agent actions, agents deserve no privacy, and new access control models are required when agents collaborate across organizational boundaries

- **Workflow transformation mandatory** - Rather than agents adapting to human work patterns, humans must restructure their workflows to make agents effective, similar to how coding practices fundamentally changed in just two years

- **"Every agent needs a Box"** - Enterprise agents require secure sandboxed environments with proper data governance, permissions management, and audit trails to prevent catastrophic security incidents from prompt injection and data leakage

- **Professional services boom incoming** - OpenAI hiring consultants and Anthropic embedding at Goldman Sachs signals that even AI labs recognize enterprise deployment requires extensive workflow re-engineering and dedicated transformation teams

- **Multi-year enterprise adoption timeline** - Early adopters gain compounding advantages, but broad enterprise transformation will take years due to data fragmentation, security requirements, and the need to retrain entire workforces on agent-centric processes
