Wolters Kluwer's new CEO Stacey Caywood argues that enterprise AI must operate in "thinking mode" — grounded in proprietary expert content and verified data rather than just advanced models — to deliver reliable value in high-stakes professional environments where hallucinations and inconsistent outputs pose unacceptable risks.

---

- **Strategic acceleration** Caywood plans to deepen AI deployment across the portfolio, expand customer ecosystem partnerships, and strengthen commercial capabilities as her three core priorities for 2026

- **Productivity pressure** Professional services firms face persistent workforce shortages, rising client demands, and need to make high-stakes decisions quickly with limited resources, driving demand for AI-powered workflow solutions

- **"Thinking mode" differentiation** Wolters Kluwer positions AI built on proprietary expert content and verified data as superior to generic models, emphasizing this approach reduces hallucinations and delivers consistent outcomes

- **Enterprise-grade requirements** High-stakes professional environments require AI with strong governance, responsible AI principles, transparency, and expert oversight rather than just technological sophistication

- **Embedded workflow advantage** The company's decade-long investment in cloud infrastructure and AI Center of Excellence, combined with deep customer workflow integration, creates competitive positioning for AI deployment

- **Trust as competitive moat** In professional services where errors carry significant consequences, reliability and expert-curated content become key differentiators over pure AI model capabilities

- **Market timing** Growing demand for agentic AI tools among professional customers creates opportunity for solutions that combine advanced technology with domain expertise and proven governance frameworks
