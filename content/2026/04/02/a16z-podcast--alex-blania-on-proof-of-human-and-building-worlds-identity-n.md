Alex Blania argues that proving human identity will become critical infrastructure as AI advances, with World positioning itself to capture massive network effects by being first to solve iris-based biometric verification at global scale. The company is building toward a future where every digital platform—from social media to financial services—will require proof-of-human verification to function properly.

---

- **Technical differentiation** World uses iris biometrics because they're uniquely stable, universal, and privacy-preserving compared to alternatives like government IDs or behavioral analysis that can be gamed by AI
- **Privacy architecture** The system generates cryptographic proofs of humanness without storing biometric data centrally, allowing verification while maintaining anonymity through zero-knowledge proofs
- **Network effects strategy** Being first to achieve global scale in human verification creates winner-take-all dynamics, as the value increases exponentially with each verified human added to the network
- **Market timing** AI's rapid advancement is creating urgent demand for human verification across platforms, with early adopters including social networks, dating apps, and financial services already integrating World ID
- **Distribution challenge** Scaling requires physical hardware (Orbs) for iris scanning, making geographic expansion and user acquisition fundamentally different from typical software companies
- **Monetization model** Revenue comes from API usage as platforms integrate World ID verification, creating recurring income streams that scale with digital platform adoption
- **Regulatory positioning** Proactive engagement with global regulators on privacy and data protection aims to establish World as the compliant standard before competitors emerge
