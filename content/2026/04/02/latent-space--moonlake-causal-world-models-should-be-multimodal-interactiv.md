Stanford's Chris Manning and Fan-yun Sun argue that current world models like Genie 3 fundamentally miss the point by focusing on video generation rather than causal understanding and long-term consistency. Their startup Moonlake is building interactive world models that use structured symbolic reasoning and game engine bootstrapping to achieve true action-conditioned prediction, betting that semantic abstraction will prove far more efficient than pixel-level scaling for spatial intelligence.

---

- **Philosophical departure** - Unlike LeCun's JEPA approach focused purely on visual learning, Manning argues language and symbolic representations are cognitive tools essential for causal reasoning and long-term planning, not just "low bit rate communication"

- **Action-conditioned models** - True world models must predict consequences of actions over minutes, not just next video frames; current video generation lacks understanding of 3D physics, object permanence, and causal relationships

- **Efficiency through structure** - Working with semantic abstractions could reduce data requirements by "five orders of magnitude" compared to pixel-level approaches, similar to how human vision processes most input top-down rather than high-resolution everywhere

- **Game engine foundation** - Bootstrapping from game engines provides ground-truth action-observation pairs that are expensive to collect from real-world video data, enabling better causal relationship learning

- **Market demand validation** - Nvidia and academic institutions already spend significant money purchasing interactive synthetic worlds for training embodied AI agents, indicating proven commercial need

- **Reasoning traces approach** - Models generate detailed symbolic reasoning about geometry, physics, affordances, and state changes before rendering, similar to language model chain-of-thought but for spatial intelligence

- **Real-time constraint advantage** - Processing limitations favor abstraction over raw pixel processing for interactive applications requiring immediate response and long-term memory maintenance

- **Multiplayer capability** - Unlike single-player video generation demos, Moonlake emphasizes indefinite runtime and multi-agent interactions from the start
