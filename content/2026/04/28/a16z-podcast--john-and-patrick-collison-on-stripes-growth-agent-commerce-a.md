Stripe's data reveals a potential economic inflection point, with 2025 businesses outperforming all previous cohorts both in quantity and quality, leading Patrick Collison to suggest 2026 Q1 could mark "the first quarter of the singularity." The company processed over $1 trillion in payments with 34% growth, driven by AI-enabled businesses that are fundamentally reshaping commerce toward agent-driven transactions requiring blockchain infrastructure capable of billions of transactions per second.

---

- **Economic Phase Transition** Stripe's 2025 business cohort is both larger and performing better per-business than any prior year, with 2026 showing potential acceleration beyond this already significant leap

- **AI Impact Validation** Despite executive surveys claiming 80% see no AI value, Stripe's transaction data shows real economic impact from AI adoption, with businesses increasingly using AI for customer service, code generation, and commerce automation

- **Agentic Commerce Infrastructure** Current web infrastructure forces agents to solve CAPTCHAs and work around human-designed systems, creating demand for native agent commerce platforms that enable direct AI-to-business transactions

- **Blockchain Scaling Requirements** The convergence of AI agents and stablecoins will require payment rails supporting billions of transactions per second—a capacity no current platform provides, driving Stripe's investment in Tempo blockchain infrastructure

- **Software Economics Transformation** AI shifts software from mass-produced, winner-take-all products to bespoke "pizza-like" creation at moment of use, fundamentally changing cost structures from fixed-cost-infinite-scale to inference-cost-per-transaction models

- **Platform Integration Strategy** Stripe is building API infrastructure with major retailers (Etsy, Shopify, Walmart, Best Buy) to enable product catalogs within AI applications, focusing on unglamorous but critical protocol work

- **Atlas Compounding Success** Stripe Atlas now powers a significant fraction of US C-Corp formations after 10 years of development, demonstrating the long-term value of solving fundamental startup infrastructure problems
