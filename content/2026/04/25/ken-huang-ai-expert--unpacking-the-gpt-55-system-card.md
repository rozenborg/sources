GPT-5.5 represents a major leap in agentic AI capabilities, delivering near-human performance in complex cybersecurity tasks and showing threefold improvements in avoiding destructive actions in real-world work environments. The model's ability to operate autonomously across tools while maintaining safety guardrails marks a critical inflection point where AI systems are becoming genuinely capable workplace agents rather than mere chatbots.

---

- **Cybersecurity escalation** OpenAI rates GPT-5.5 as "High" capability in cybersecurity, approaching but not yet reaching "Critical" threshold where models can autonomously develop zero-day exploits against hardened systems

- **Destructive action prevention** Perfect reversion score improved threefold from 0.18 to 0.52, meaning the model is significantly better at undoing only its own changes while preserving user work in shared environments

- **Agentic architecture** Uses internal chain-of-thought reasoning with test-time compute scaling in Pro version, designed for complex multi-step tasks across tools rather than simple question-answering

- **Safety regression risks** Notable drops in extremism compliance (1.000 to 0.925) and self-harm handling (0.977 to 0.937) demonstrate the challenge of maintaining safety across all categories simultaneously

- **Hallucination paradox** Individual claims are 23% more accurate than GPT-5.4, but responses contain factual errors only 3% less often due to increased verbosity creating more opportunities for mistakes

- **Medical performance gains** Length-adjusted HealthBench Professional scores improved 3.7 points to 51.8, indicating stronger capability for clinical use cases

- **Real-world deployment readiness** Nearly 200 early-access partners tested the model on actual use cases, with rigorous pre-deployment safety evaluations including targeted red-teaming for advanced capabilities
