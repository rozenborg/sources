AI is becoming incredibly efficient at generating emails, presentations, and documents, but most organizations can't clearly define what actual value their work creates beyond these outputs. Like morning commuters who discovered milkshakes solved their "long boring drive" problem better than bananas or donuts, we need to identify the real job our work is hired to do before AI optimization becomes just busy work at scale.

---

- **The Milkshake Principle** - Clayton Christensen's research revealed milkshakes weren't bought as desserts but as solutions for boring commutes, showing products succeed when they fulfill the actual job customers need done, not the assumed purpose

- **Articulation Gap Crisis** - Most organizational expertise lives in people's heads (the "Cliff problem"), and AI is exposing how much work runs on "vibes and spreadsheets" rather than documented, measurable processes

- **Output vs. Outcome Confusion** - Teams are using AI tools like Claude CoWork to create massive volumes of emails, decks, and documents without questioning whether these deliverables actually move strategic needles

- **Jobs-to-be-Done Framework** - Organizations must identify the real job their work performs for stakeholders (clients, shareholders, public) rather than focusing on activity metrics like token utilization or document production

- **Professional Excellence vs. Task Completion** - The author's realization that their job isn't just "helping people learn AI" but "helping people achieve professional excellence" demonstrates the depth of analysis needed for each role

- **Measurability Revolution** - AI is making actual work quality, costs, and value visible in ways that were never practical before, forcing organizations to confront the gap between effort and impact

- **Strategic Recalibration Required** - Leaders need clear maps of both the jobs-to-be-done and execution methods before deploying AI agents, or risk automating the wrong activities
