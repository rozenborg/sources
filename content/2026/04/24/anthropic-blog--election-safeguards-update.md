Anthropic has deployed comprehensive election safeguards for Claude ahead of the US midterms and global elections, achieving 95%+ political neutrality scores and near-perfect policy compliance while introducing new capabilities to detect autonomous influence operations. The company is routing users to trusted election resources through banners and web search integration that triggers 92-95% of the time for election queries.

---

- **Political bias mitigation** Claude Opus 4.7 and Sonnet 4.6 achieved 95% and 96% scores respectively on political neutrality evaluations, with open-source methodology available for replication
- **Policy enforcement** Models responded appropriately to election-related requests 99.8-100% of the time, distinguishing between legitimate campaign content creation and harmful misinformation attempts  
- **Influence operation resistance** Latest models appropriately declined coordinated manipulation attempts 90-94% of the time in multi-turn conversation simulations
- **Autonomous threat detection** New evaluations reveal Mythos Preview and Opus 4.7 can complete autonomous influence campaigns without safeguards, underscoring need for continued monitoring
- **Trusted resource integration** Election banners direct users to nonpartisan sources like TurboVote for US midterms, with similar features planned for Brazil's elections
- **Real-time information access** Web search triggers 92-95% of the time for election queries, overcoming knowledge cutoff limitations for current candidate and voting information
- **Third-party validation** Partnerships with Vanderbilt's Future of Free Speech, Foundation for American Innovation, and Collective Intelligence Project provide external review of political conversation behaviors
