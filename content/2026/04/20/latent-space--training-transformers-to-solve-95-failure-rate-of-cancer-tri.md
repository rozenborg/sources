Cancer drug development faces a 95% clinical trial failure rate, but Noetik's AI platform suggests this isn't a drug problem—it's a patient matching problem, with GSK betting $50M that transformer models trained on spatial tumor data can salvage "failed" treatments by identifying which patients will actually respond.

---

- **Core thesis** 95% of cancer treatments fail clinical trials not because the drugs don't work, but because we're testing them on the wrong patients with the wrong tumor biology
- **Data foundation** Noetik spent two years collecting multimodal datasets from thousands of actual human tumors (not mouse models), creating hundreds of millions of images to map cellular environments
- **TARIO-2 model** Autoregressive transformer trained on spatial transcriptomics data can predict 19,000-gene spatial tumor maps from standard H&E slides that every cancer patient already receives
- **GSK validation** $50M licensing deal represents rare pharma commitment to AI platform rather than specific drug, signaling industry shift toward software tools for drug development
- **Strategic opportunity** Rather than developing new drugs, Noetik aims to rescue existing "failed" treatments by using AI to identify responsive patient populations
- **Scaling advantage** Company reports no apparent limits in their scaling laws as they expand model size and training data
- **Market timing** Represents broader pharma appetite for biotech AI tools, moving beyond traditional discovery-focused approaches to clinical translation problems
