Most firms fail at AI adoption because their valuable institutional knowledge remains buried in unsearchable documents and siloed systems, while leadership treats implementation as an IT project rather than a fundamental business transformation requiring senior ownership, realistic timelines, and organizational restructuring.

---

- **Data accessibility crisis** - Institutional IP trapped in findable presentations and memos creates a garbage-in-garbage-out problem where AI agents can't access what humans can't locate
- **Ownership misalignment** - Companies treat AI deployment as IT tickets instead of P&L initiatives requiring senior executive responsibility for data curation and strategy
- **Timeline reality check** - Organizations consistently underestimate implementation timelines while overestimating data readiness, leading to failed expectations and stalled projects
- **Organizational structure bottleneck** - The company org chart poses bigger deployment challenges than technical limitations, requiring fundamental workflow and responsibility redesigns
- **Context curation imperative** - Success requires dedicated senior resources for corpus management and anonymization workflows before tool procurement, not after
- **Fragmentation challenge** - Non-technical users struggle with AI tools that don't integrate across their existing fragmented data sources and workflows
- **Strategic positioning shift** - Enterprise AI adoption creates a flywheel effect once initial obstacles are overcome, but requires treating it as business transformation rather than technology upgrade
