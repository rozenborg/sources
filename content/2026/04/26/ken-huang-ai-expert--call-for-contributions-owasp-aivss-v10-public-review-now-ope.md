OWASP has launched the public review for AIVSS v1.0, the emerging standard for scoring security risks in agentic AI systems, following successful showcasing at RSAC 2026 with participation from NSA, NIST, and Anthropic leadership. The framework aims to become the definitive open-source tool for enterprise AI risk assessment, with final publication scheduled before end of 2026.

---

- **Review Timeline** Public comment period runs until October 1, 2026, with early contributions having greatest impact on the final v1.0 framework scheduled for publication before year-end

- **Industry Validation** RSAC 2026 panel featured Rob Joyce (former NSA Cybersecurity Director), Jason Clinton (Anthropic Deputy CISO), and NIST's Apostol Vassilev, demonstrating high-level government and industry backing

- **Framework Integration** AIVSS aligns with major standards including NIST AI RMF, OWASP GenAI/LLM Top 10, and CSA MAESTRO, positioning it as a comprehensive risk management bridge across existing frameworks

- **Enterprise Application** Real-world deployment evidence from pharmaceutical sector shows the framework's viability in highly regulated environments where AI risk management is critical

- **Contribution Areas** Seeking input on quantitative risk models, attack scenarios, SSVC decision-tree prioritization, and regulatory alignment from security professionals and AI practitioners

- **Previous Success** Version 0.8 incorporated over 1,900 public comments and achieved co-publication with multiple OWASP projects, indicating strong community engagement and collaborative development process

- **Strategic Positioning** Framework targets the specific security challenges of agentic AI systems rather than general AI risks, addressing a gap in current security standards as autonomous AI deployment accelerates
