Decision-making by consensus, the dominant management approach of the past 50 years, is becoming a fatal liability in the AI era as it simply cannot match the speed and decisiveness required to compete. The companies that will survive the next decade won't be distinguished by superior algorithms or data, but by their willingness to fundamentally restructure how they make decisions.

---

- **Speed mismatch** Consensus-based processes are inherently slow, requiring buy-in from multiple stakeholders while AI-driven markets demand rapid response times

- **Competitive disadvantage** Organizations clinging to collaborative decision-making will be systematically outmaneuvered by competitors using more centralized, agile approaches

- **False security** Most leaders acknowledge AI's transformative impact but resist admitting their decision-making structures are obsolete, creating dangerous blind spots

- **Survival criteria shift** Technical capabilities like algorithms and data access are becoming commoditized; organizational agility is the new differentiator

- **Leadership courage required** The transition demands executives actively dismantle established consensus cultures, not just implement new technologies

- **Organizational reckoning** Companies face a fundamental choice between preserving comfortable collaborative norms and adapting to AI-speed business environments

- **Implementation urgency** The window for making this transition is narrowing as AI adoption accelerates across industries
