Peter Yang argues that AI agents will eliminate most traditional apps by handling tasks proactively, while coding agents will make software development so cheap that future companies will operate with radically smaller teams, fundamentally reshaping how we work and build products.

---

- **AI agents replacing apps** Personal AI assistants will proactively handle routine tasks like booking travel or scheduling, making most current mobile apps obsolete as intermediaries
- **Coding agents as slot machines** Current AI coding tools create addictive workflows where developers repeatedly prompt for solutions, but this will evolve into more sophisticated automated development processes  
- **Near-zero software costs** When AI can build software cheaply and quickly, the economic barriers to creating new products will collapse, democratizing entrepreneurship
- **Radically smaller teams** Future successful companies will operate with far fewer employees as AI handles most operational and development work, concentrating human effort on strategy and creativity
- **IDE evolution** Integrated development environments are transforming from tools for writing code into platforms for thinking through problems and directing AI agents
- **Unlimited human ambition** Despite AI automation, human desire to create, compete, and solve new problems will continuously generate demand for work that didn't previously exist
- **Roblox scaling insights** Yang's experience scaling products at Roblox demonstrates how platforms can enable millions of creators when technical barriers are lowered
