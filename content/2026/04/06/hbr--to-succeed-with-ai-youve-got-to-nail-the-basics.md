Organizations are failing with AI implementations because they're using artificial intelligence as a band-aid over fundamentally broken processes and poor-quality data, creating amplified problems instead of solutions. The fantasy that AI will mask operational deficiencies is leading to worse outcomes than the original manual systems.

---

- **Data quality delusion** - Companies assume their existing data is "good enough" for AI despite years of underinvestment in data infrastructure and governance
- **Process foundation failure** - AI amplifies existing operational problems rather than hiding them, making broken workflows more visible and costly
- **Human oversight misconception** - Organizations overestimate their ability to catch and correct AI errors through manual review, creating false security
- **Garbage amplification effect** - Poor inputs don't just produce poor outputs with AI; they generate systematically flawed results at scale and speed
- **Investment sequencing error** - Companies are skipping essential groundwork (data cleanup, process optimization) to jump directly to AI implementation
- **Risk multiplication** - AI systems built on weak foundations create new categories of customer dissatisfaction and operational risk beyond traditional failure modes
- **Prerequisites ignored** - Successful AI deployment requires fixing basic data management, process documentation, and quality control systems first
