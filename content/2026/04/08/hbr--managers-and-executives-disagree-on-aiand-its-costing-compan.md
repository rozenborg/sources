Corporations are caught in an expensive AI commitment trap where executives continue pumping massive budgets into artificial intelligence initiatives while middle managers on the ground question the practical value and timeline for meaningful returns. This executive-manager disconnect is creating organizational friction and potentially wasting millions in AI investments that may not deliver promised transformation outcomes.

---

- **Budget-Reality Gap** Large corporations have moved beyond pilot programs to major AI budget commitments, but ground-level managers see limited practical applications delivering measurable business value

- **Timeline Misalignment** C-suite executives expect rapid AI transformation results while operational managers deal with implementation challenges that suggest much longer deployment timelines

- **Resource Allocation Conflict** Companies are allocating significant capital to AI initiatives based on executive vision rather than validated use cases identified by managers closest to day-to-day operations

- **Change Management Crisis** The disconnect between executive AI enthusiasm and managerial skepticism creates internal resistance that could sabotage implementation efforts and waste invested resources

- **Competitive Pressure Driving Decisions** Organizations may be committing to AI investments based on competitive positioning rather than clear business cases, risking substantial financial losses

- **Skills and Training Disconnect** Executive AI commitments may not align with the actual workforce capabilities and training needs identified by managers responsible for execution
