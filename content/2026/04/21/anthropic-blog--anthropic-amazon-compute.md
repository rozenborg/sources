Anthropic secures massive infrastructure expansion with Amazon through a $100+ billion, 10-year commitment for up to 5 gigawatts of compute capacity, while Amazon increases its total investment to potentially $33 billion as Anthropic's revenue run-rate surges to $30 billion amid infrastructure strain from unprecedented demand growth.

---

- **Massive compute expansion** Up to 5GW of new AWS capacity secured through $100+ billion commitment over 10 years, including Trainium2-4 chips with nearly 1GW coming online by end of 2026
- **Revenue explosion** Run-rate revenue jumped from ~$9 billion at end of 2025 to over $30 billion currently, driven by enterprise adoption and consumer usage surge across all tiers
- **Infrastructure strain acknowledged** Rapid growth has impacted reliability and performance for all user tiers during peak hours, prompting this major capacity expansion to address bottlenecks
- **Deepened financial partnership** Amazon's total investment reaches up to $33 billion ($8B previous + $5B today + potential $20B future), solidifying strategic alignment
- **Platform integration** Full Claude Platform launching directly within AWS with unified billing and governance, currently in private beta with existing customer access
- **Global expansion planned** Agreement includes inference capacity expansion in Asia and Europe to serve growing international customer base
- **Multi-cloud strategy maintained** Claude remains available across all three major cloud platforms (AWS Bedrock, Google Cloud Vertex AI, Microsoft Azure Foundry) despite deepened AWS partnership
- **Immediate relief timeline** Meaningful compute capacity expected within three months to address current performance issues
