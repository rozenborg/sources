Venture capital has evolved from a small, relationship-based cottage industry into a scalable system for funding technology companies, with AI now fundamentally reshaping both how firms operate and what kinds of companies can be built at unprecedented speed and scale.

---

- **Industry transformation** - VC shifted from personal relationships and small deal flow to systematic approaches that can evaluate and fund technology companies at scale across global markets

- **Network effects advantage** - Successful VC firms create powerful network effects where portfolio companies, entrepreneurs, and industry connections reinforce each other, making top firms increasingly dominant

- **Firm design evolution** - Modern VC firms must balance maintaining entrepreneurial culture and decision-making speed while building scalable processes and specialized expertise across different sectors

- **AI capital intensity** - AI companies require significantly more upfront capital than previous technology waves, fundamentally changing the venture funding landscape and favoring firms with larger funds

- **Speed of company building** - AI tools are dramatically accelerating how quickly new companies can be built and scaled, compressing traditional development timelines and changing competitive dynamics

- **Leadership in uncertainty** - Successful venture investing requires leaders who can make decisions with incomplete information while building cultures that attract top talent in highly competitive markets

- **Portfolio leverage** - The most successful firms use their portfolio companies as a platform to identify new opportunities and provide strategic advantages to portfolio companies through cross-connections
