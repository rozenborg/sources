Applied Intuition has evolved from YC-era autonomy tooling into a $15B physical AI company with 1,000 engineers deploying AI systems onto safety-critical machines across automotive, mining, construction, agriculture, and defense. Unlike screen-based AI that can make mistakes in chat applications, their systems power driverless L4 trucks running in Japan today where errors could be catastrophic, requiring fundamentally different approaches to reliability, latency, and real-time control.

---

- **Physical AI vs. Screen AI** - Safety-critical systems like autonomous vehicles need drastically higher reliability than chatbots; learned systems must handle real-world consequences where mistakes can cause physical harm rather than just incorrect text output

- **Three Technology Buckets** - Applied Intuition operates across simulation/RL infrastructure for testing, true operating systems for real-time vehicle control, and fundamental AI models for autonomy and world understanding across multiple machine types

- **Operating Systems Gap** - Current vehicle software stacks are fragmented like "phones before Android," requiring real-time control, millisecond sensor streaming, memory management, and reliable over-the-air updates without "bricking" vehicles

- **Deployment Bottleneck** - The constraint is no longer model intelligence but deploying AI onto hardware under safety, latency, power, and cost requirements; onboard models need millisecond response times while data center models can be large and slow

- **Statistical Safety Evolution** - Autonomy validation is shifting from binary pass/fail tests toward "how many nines" of reliability and mean time between failures, requiring new approaches to verification as systems become more capable

- **Production vs. Demos** - After nearly a decade, Applied Intuition can predict the "next 20 problems" any robotics demo will encounter in productionization; the gap between research demos and deployment-ready systems remains enormous

- **Market Positioning** - With 18 of top 20 global non-Chinese automakers as customers, they position as a technology provider like NVIDIA but for physical AI software rather than chips, selling across the spectrum from individual tools to full autonomous systems
