The AI gold rush is ending as Anthropic shifts from generous flat-rate pricing to expensive pay-per-use tokens, effectively killing popular applications like OpenClaw that relied on subsidized access to powerful models. What was once a $100 monthly unlimited buffet now costs power users $3,000 per month, forcing a fundamental reckoning with AI economics across both individual users and enterprise IT departments.

---

- **End of AI subsidies** Anthropic terminated the generous token limits on $100-200 monthly Claude plans, mirroring Uber's transition from VC-subsidized cheap rides to profitable pricing that increased costs 4-5x over a decade

- **OpenClaw casualty** The popular open-source AI agent with millions of downloads became financially unviable, with power users facing $50-100 daily bills ($1,500-3,000 monthly) instead of flat subscription fees

- **Enterprise budget constraints** Corporate IT departments are establishing token budgets for teams, creating a new decision layer that may stifle AI experimentation and creative use cases during the critical adoption phase

- **Token anxiety emergence** Pay-per-use pricing creates psychological barriers to experimentation while simultaneously making token cost optimization a required skill for the near future

- **ROI threshold established** Goldman Sachs CIO Marco Argenti set the benchmark: AI usage remains profitable as long as token cost per hour stays below wage cost per hour

- **Optimization strategies required** Users must shift to cheaper Sonnet models, avoid long conversations, manage context through projects, and use scripts to process large files efficiently

- **Strategic inflection point** Organizations face 3,700+ leaders hitting usage limits, forcing decisions between unlimited AI access and cost control as the technology transitions from experimental to operational
