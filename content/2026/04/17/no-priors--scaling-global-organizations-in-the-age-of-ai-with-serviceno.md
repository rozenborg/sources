ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott argues that AI should function as a "control tower" for enterprise operations, orchestrating workflows across systems rather than replacing human judgment, while generative AI fundamentally reshapes how enterprises approach workforce planning and customer service delivery.

---

- **Early Leadership Philosophy** McDermott's approach stems from running a deli at 16, emphasizing that successful leadership requires treating every stakeholder—employees, customers, suppliers—as essential to the business ecosystem

- **AI as Orchestration Layer** ServiceNow positions AI not as a replacement technology but as a control tower that coordinates and optimizes workflows across multiple enterprise systems, making complex organizations more efficient

- **Platform vs. Point Solutions** McDermott distinguishes ServiceNow as a platform business that connects disparate enterprise functions, arguing this integration approach will survive AI disruption better than narrow SaaS tools

- **Workforce Transformation Timeline** AI agents will reshape white-collar work within 2-3 years, with enterprises needing to rethink job roles, training programs, and organizational structures rather than simply automating existing processes

- **Implementation Speed Advantage** AI is dramatically reducing enterprise software deployment times from months to weeks, giving companies that master rapid implementation a significant competitive edge

- **Customer Conversation Shift** Enterprise AI discussions have evolved from "what is AI?" to "how do we measure ROI and scale AI initiatives across our organization?"

- **Cultural Resilience Strategy** Building company culture that survives technological shifts requires maintaining human connection and agency while using technology to amplify rather than replace human capabilities
