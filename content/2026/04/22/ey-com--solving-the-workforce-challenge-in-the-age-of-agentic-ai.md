EY identifies a critical workforce transformation challenge where manufacturers must capture retiring workers' tacit knowledge and fundamentally restructure their approach to human-AI collaboration, positioning this as the key differentiator for companies seeking to disrupt traditional labor cost structures in manufacturing operations.

---

- **Tacit Knowledge Crisis** EY survey identifies labor/skill shortages as manufacturers' top 12-24 month priority, with companies discovering too late that standard procedures don't reflect actual worker expertise when veterans retire

- **Operational Transformation** Production processes will compress from 12-step to 4-step workflows as AI agents handle autonomous decision-making, fundamentally changing operator skill requirements from manual assessment to AI supervision

- **Cultural Shift Required** Success demands moving beyond simply applying new tools to existing processes toward reimagining production workflows that blend human expertise with autonomous AI capabilities

- **Knowledge Capture Technology** Recent AI developments offer new solutions for converting workers' experiential knowledge into standardized, automated decision intelligence systems

- **Hybrid Workforce Management** Managers must develop entirely new capabilities to hire, upskill, and supervise human-AI teams while learning to interrogate AI reasoning processes

- **Competitive Advantage** Winners will be manufacturers who successfully disrupt traditional labor cost structures by optimally balancing "brainpower and bot power" rather than just improving existing output

- **Strategic Timing** Material cost pressures create urgency for manufacturers to act now on workforce transformation before competitors gain sustainable advantages through better human-AI integration
