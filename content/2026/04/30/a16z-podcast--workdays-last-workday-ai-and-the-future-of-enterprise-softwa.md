Workday and other legacy enterprise software giants face potential obsolescence as AI-native competitors emerge, with their complex, rigid systems becoming increasingly vulnerable to agent-based platforms that could fundamentally rewrite how companies handle workflows and permissions. Current enterprise software "AI revenue" is largely cosmetic feature additions rather than true transformation, leaving incumbents exposed to complete replatforming by startups building from scratch.

---

- **Legacy SaaS limitations** - Workday and similar platforms suffer from complex permission systems, rigid workflows, and user interfaces designed for 2010s technology that feel increasingly broken in modern work environments

- **AI revenue misconception** - Most enterprise software companies claiming "AI revenue" are simply adding chatbot interfaces and basic automation features rather than rebuilding their core architecture for AI-native functionality

- **Agent-based disruption** - Future enterprise systems will likely center on AI agents that can navigate permissions, execute workflows, and manage data across systems without requiring human interface manipulation

- **Platform shift vulnerability** - Even highly defensible enterprise software businesses with strong switching costs face existential threat from complete replatforming, similar to how mobile disrupted desktop software

- **Ground-up opportunity** - Startups building AI-native enterprise platforms from scratch have architectural advantages over incumbents trying to retrofit AI onto legacy codebases and data models

- **Workflow reimagination** - Next-generation enterprise software will likely eliminate traditional concepts like complex approval chains and manual data entry in favor of intelligent, context-aware automation

- **Replatforming timeline** - While enterprise software replacement cycles are typically slow, AI capabilities may accelerate adoption of new platforms that offer dramatically superior user experiences
