Anthropic introduces the Long-Term Benefit Trust, an independent five-member body that will gradually gain authority to elect a majority of Anthropic's board within four years, designed to ensure AI development decisions balance public benefit with shareholder interests during high-stakes scenarios. This experimental governance structure acknowledges that transformative AI may create unprecedented externalities requiring corporate accountability beyond traditional shareholder-focused models.

---

- **Phased Power Transfer** The Trust will progressively gain board control through time and funding milestones, ultimately electing a majority within 4 years while creating a new investor-elected seat to maintain stakeholder representation

- **Class T Stock Creation** Anthropic amended its charter to create special stock held exclusively by the Trust, granting board election authority and protective provisions requiring Trust notification of significant corporate changes

- **High-Stakes Focus** The LTBT is designed primarily for extreme scenarios and long-range decisions rather than day-to-day operations, such as evaluating catastrophic AI risks or ensuring nation-state level security over speed-to-market pressures

- **Financial Insulation** Trustees are financially disinterested in Anthropic and serve one-year terms, with future trustees elected by existing trustees to maintain independence from commercial pressures

- **Experimental Framework** The structure includes "failsafe" provisions allowing stockholder supermajorities to modify the Trust if needed, with higher thresholds as Trust power increases, balancing durability with iterative improvement

- **Expert Composition** Initial trustees include leaders from RAND Corporation, Evidence Action, Clinton Health Access Initiative, Alignment Research Center, and Effective Ventures US, bringing AI safety, policy, and social enterprise expertise

- **Legal Innovation** Built on Delaware Public Benefit Corporation status and purpose trust law, this represents a novel approach to addressing AI governance challenges that existing corporate law wasn't designed to handle
