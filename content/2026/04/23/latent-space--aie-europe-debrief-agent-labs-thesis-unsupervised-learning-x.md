The AI infrastructure landscape is finally stabilizing around consensus patterns like "skills" (simple markdown files with scripts) for agent integration, while domain-specific model training by companies like Cursor and Cognition is proving to be real value-add rather than just marketing positioning. The 2026 thesis predicts coding agents will "break containment" and expand beyond code into general business tasks, potentially disrupting traditional SaaS as companies increasingly choose AI-native replacements over expensive legacy software.

---

- **Infrastructure stabilization** Companies like LangChain have stopped reinventing themselves yearly as building patterns converge around minimal viable formats for agent integration, though application companies still have advantages over pure infrastructure plays due to stickier end customers

- **Agent lab playbook validated** Start with frontier models, specialize for your domain, then train custom models once you have sufficient data and workload - both Cursor and Cognition users actively choose their in-house models in fair market comparisons

- **Alternative inference acceleration** Non-NVIDIA chips like Cerebras are delivering thousands of tokens per second versus sub-100, with every 10x speedup unlocking entirely new application categories and usage patterns

- **Agents as primary customers** 60% of Vercel's admin traffic is now bots rather than humans, making API-first design and excellent documentation critical rather than optional - essentially making agent experience equivalent to good developer experience

- **Pre-training data incumbents compound** Companies mentioned in training data before 2023 have persistent advantages, though future memory and personalization systems will likely override current frequency-based selection criteria

- **Coding market structure emerging** The AI coding category shows clear winners (Cursor, Claude, Copilot) with sticky first magical experiences, potentially previewing how other vertical AI markets will develop with two major players plus a long tail

- **SaaS displacement accelerating** Internal tensions between AI-native employees wanting to replace expensive software and skeptics worried about fragile custom systems, with traditional SaaS facing real pressure from cheap AI-built alternatives

- **Memory as the bottleneck** Context windows remain the slowest-scaling factor in AI systems, with million-token context still not changing most real workflows despite being the key limitation for next-generation capabilities
