SAP's Chief Technology Officer Philipp Herzig argues that successful enterprise AI adoption hinges on four critical areas: customer outcomes, UI transformation, business process integration, and robust data infrastructure, while traditional predictive tabular models are hitting fundamental limitations that require new approaches. As the "operating system" for over 400,000 companies, SAP is positioning itself to bridge the enterprise AI adoption gap by focusing on verifiable, secure implementations rather than flashy demos.

---

- **Enterprise AI Focus Areas** - Herzig identifies customer outcomes, UI changes, business processes, and data layer as the four pillars where AI will transform enterprise operations, moving beyond surface-level implementations

- **Predictive Model Limitations** - Traditional tabular prediction models are reaching diminishing returns due to fundamental constraints in how businesses operate and make decisions, requiring new AI approaches

- **Verifiability Over Demos** - Enterprise AI adoption requires robust evaluation frameworks and verifiable results rather than impressive demonstrations, with security and scaling as primary technical barriers

- **Data Fragmentation Challenge** - Enterprise customers struggle with fragmented data across systems, making AI implementation complex and requiring sophisticated integration strategies

- **Pricing Model Evolution** - SAP is shifting from traditional licensing to outcome-based and consumption-based pricing models to align with AI-driven value creation

- **Agent Mining Innovation** - SAP is developing "agent mining" capabilities that observe and learn from business processes to automate decision-making, moving beyond simple task automation

- **Quantum Computing Investment** - Herzig sees quantum computing as a medium-term opportunity for optimization problems in supply chain and logistics, where classical computing hits mathematical limits
