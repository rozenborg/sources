Ben Horowitz argues that AI has fundamentally broken traditional software economics by eliminating marginal cost advantages and creating new winner-take-all dynamics based on data scale and infrastructure rather than code efficiency. The shift means venture capital must adapt to funding companies that require massive upfront infrastructure investments before achieving traditional software margins.

---

- **Traditional software economics disrupted** - AI eliminates the classic advantage of zero marginal costs since AI inference requires significant compute resources for each transaction, fundamentally changing unit economics

- **New competitive moats emerging** - Success now depends on data scale, infrastructure capacity, and training capabilities rather than pure software development speed or feature differentiation

- **Infrastructure becomes the battleground** - Companies that control the underlying AI infrastructure stack (compute, training, inference) will capture disproportionate value compared to application-layer players

- **Crypto integration necessity** - Blockchain infrastructure will become essential for AI systems to handle payments, identity verification, and trustless transactions at the scale and speed AI applications require

- **VC model evolution required** - Traditional venture funding approaches must adapt to support companies needing massive capital for infrastructure before reaching software-like margins and scalability

- **Winner-take-all intensifies** - The combination of high infrastructure costs and data network effects creates even stronger winner-take-all dynamics than previous software generations

- **Timing advantage compressed** - First-mover advantages matter less when infrastructure requirements are so high that only well-funded players can compete effectively from the start
