Coding has emerged as AI's first legitimate breakout application, but the fundamental questions about where value will be captured in the AI ecosystem remain unresolved, with the current infrastructure spending boom masking uncertainty about long-term software economics and adoption patterns.

---

- **Coding as breakthrough use case** - AI-powered coding assistance represents the first clear, measurable productivity gain that justifies widespread enterprise adoption, unlike previous AI applications that struggled to demonstrate concrete value

- **Infrastructure vs. application value capture** - The massive spending on AI infrastructure (chips, data centers, foundation models) may not translate to sustainable profits if AI becomes commoditized infrastructure rather than defensible application layer value

- **Enterprise adoption patterns** - Current AI adoption follows familiar enterprise software cycles where early productivity tools gain traction first, but transformative workflow changes require longer timeframes and organizational restructuring

- **Foundation model commoditization risk** - The proliferation of capable AI models suggests that differentiation may shift away from the model layer toward applications, data access, and specialized implementations

- **Software economics disruption** - AI agents capable of generating code could fundamentally alter software development costs and pricing models, though the timeline and extent of this disruption remains uncertain

- **Consumer vs. enterprise divergence** - While enterprise AI adoption shows clear ROI in specific use cases like coding, consumer AI applications still lack compelling mass-market value propositions beyond novelty

- **Platform shift parallels** - The current AI moment resembles previous platform transitions (mobile, cloud) where initial infrastructure investment preceded application innovation, but AI's unique characteristics make historical analogies imperfect
