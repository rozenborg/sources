AI skills files—portable prompt packages that automate complex workflows—are becoming essential productivity tools, but most professionals struggle to identify use cases and create effective implementations. A new library of 11 ready-to-use skills targeting investment professionals demonstrates how to systematically build these automation tools through structured interviewing, domain expertise validation, and iterative testing.

---

- **Skills Library Categories** Three focus areas: Meeting/Inbox Prep (triage, briefings, call follow-up), Investment Research (public/private company analysis, hedge fund diligence), and Investor Relations (LP updates, marketing materials, outreach templates)

- **Creation Methodology** Four-step process: brainstorm use cases against recurring pain points, use interview method to refine scope before building, leverage existing skill-creator tools for file generation, then rigorously test outputs against domain expertise

- **Security and Customization Risk** Third-party skills pose prompt injection vulnerabilities and outsource critical domain expertise—use external skills as inspiration templates rather than final implementations to maintain control over proprietary workflows

- **Implementation Friction** Current skill deployment requires cumbersome manual processes (download, folder creation, zipping, upload) that limit mainstream adoption, though workarounds exist through direct file uploads to chat sessions

- **Domain Expertise Requirement** Effective skill validation depends entirely on subject matter knowledge—skills codify existing expertise rather than replace it, making personal workflow understanding essential for quality control

- **Claude Cowork Integration** Dedicated platform offers streamlined skill management with Microsoft 365, Google Suite, and Slack connectors for automated meeting prep, inbox management, and data organization workflows

- **Testing and Validation Critical** Skills require extensive output verification against intended use cases—poorly tested skills generate inaccurate, misformatted, or incomplete results that undermine productivity gains
