Anthropic's first major public opinion survey of 52,000 Americans reveals a massive trust deficit, with only 15% trusting AI companies to make development decisions while 71% want government regulation. Americans overwhelmingly fear job displacement (64%) but see disease cures as AI's greatest promise (48%), creating political consensus around accountability over corporate self-governance.

---

- **Trust Crisis**: Only 15% of Americans trust AI companies with development decisions—lower than federal government (20%) or any other institution tested, signaling major reputational challenges for the industry

- **Bipartisan Regulatory Appetite**: 71% support government AI involvement (79% Democrats, 68% Republicans), with majority backing across all states, particularly for privacy protection (56%) and child safety (52%)

- **Job Loss Dominates Fears**: 64% worry about AI job displacement across all demographics and states, with higher-educated workers paradoxically more concerned despite being early adopters

- **Experience Reduces Anxiety**: Daily AI users are significantly less worried about job loss (54% vs 70%) and cognitive dependency (46% vs 62%), suggesting hands-on experience builds confidence

- **Accountability Over Innovation**: 47% want legal liability for AI companies and 44% prioritize safety over growth, indicating public preference for restraint over rapid advancement

- **Healthcare Hope**: Nearly half (48%) see disease cures as AI's top potential benefit, 12 points ahead of disability assistance (36%), showing clear priorities for beneficial applications

- **Strategic Intelligence Gap**: Survey reveals disconnect between industry self-perception and public sentiment, with even heavy AI users (6% of population) supporting regulation at national rates despite higher company trust
