Anthropic is expanding its Project Glasswing cybersecurity initiative from 50 to 200 partner organizations across 15+ countries, giving critical infrastructure providers access to its Claude Mythos Preview AI model that has already identified over 10,000 high-severity vulnerabilities. The company warns that similar AI cyber capabilities will become widely available within 6-12 months from competitors potentially without safeguards, creating an urgent need for defenders to adapt before attackers gain access to these tools.

---

- **Partner expansion** Project Glasswing grows from ~50 to ~200 organizations spanning power, water, healthcare, communications, and hardware sectors, with each partner's potential breach affecting 100+ million people
- **Vulnerability discovery scale** Initial partners have identified over 10,000 high or critical-severity security flaws using Claude Mythos Preview for codebase scanning
- **Competitive timeline pressure** Anthropic expects other AI companies will release Mythos-class cyber models within 6-12 months, potentially without misuse safeguards
- **Public product launch** Claude Security now available using Claude Opus 4.8 for codebase scanning and patch suggestions, with specialized tools provided to trusted security teams
- **Bottleneck shift** Primary cybersecurity challenge has moved from finding vulnerabilities to verifying, disclosing, and patching the large volumes AI models can surface
- **Safeguard development gap** Anthropic acknowledges they and other AI developers lack sufficiently robust safeguards to prevent misuse of cyber capabilities for general release
- **Strategic defensive positioning** Company aims to create "permanent advantage for defenders" by providing early access to advanced AI cyber tools before attackers obtain similar capabilities
