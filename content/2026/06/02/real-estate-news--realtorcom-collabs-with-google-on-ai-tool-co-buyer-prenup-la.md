Realtor.com has partnered with Google to launch RealAssist AI, a conversational search tool that maps daily routines against properties and provides personalized affordability breakdowns, signaling the platform's push to become the dominant AI-powered home search destination. Meanwhile, new tools from Cloze enable brokerages to build custom apps without development teams, and Pairgap introduces legal frameworks for co-buying arrangements, reflecting the industry's broader shift toward specialized, AI-driven solutions for complex real estate challenges.

---

- **Realtor.com's AI strategy** Google Maps AI powers RealAssist's ability to map user routines against properties, helping buyers assess lifestyle fit before touring homes, positioning Realtor.com as the premium AI search platform
- **Moving cost integration** Partnership with Agoyu enables 30-second video scans to calculate moving expenses and generate vetted company quotes, expanding Realtor.com's transaction ecosystem beyond search
- **Brokerage autonomy tools** Cloze Forge allows brokerages to build branded apps with zero-trust security and AI development capabilities, reducing dependence on vendor updates while maintaining brand control
- **Co-ownership legal framework** Pairgap's Real Estate Prenup Builder addresses the growing co-buyer market with attorney-reviewed agreements covering ownership percentages and exit procedures
- **Workflow consolidation** Realty ONE's ZONE Pro platform combines predictive AI with referral management, reflecting franchisor efforts to provide comprehensive business management tools
- **NAR's innovation focus** Selection of six REACH program companies targeting data fragmentation and compliance automation indicates industry priority areas for technology investment
- **Market positioning** Multiple platforms launching AI-powered personalization features suggests intensifying competition for consumer engagement in the home search process
