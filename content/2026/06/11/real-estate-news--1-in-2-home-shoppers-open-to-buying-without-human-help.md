Real estate is approaching a digital inflection point as half of prospective homebuyers express willingness to purchase homes without direct human involvement, with nearly 90% ready to share financial data with AI mortgage tools. This represents a fundamental shift in consumer comfort with AI for high-stakes financial decisions, though complete AI adoption remains years away with only 25% expressing strong confidence in fully automated transactions.

---

- **Consumer AI Acceptance Surge** 53% of prospective buyers comfortable with AI-assisted home purchases, with 89% willing to share financial information for personalized mortgage guidance
- **Military Leads Adoption** Veterans show 77% trust in AI mortgage advice versus 59% of civilians, indicating demographic-specific technology adoption patterns
- **Financial Services Prime for Disruption** 68% trust AI-provided mortgage information, suggesting lending and financing functions may see faster AI integration than other real estate services
- **Agent Role Remains Dominant** Despite AI openness, 88-91% of actual buyers and sellers still use human agents, creating a gap between stated preferences and current behavior
- **Cautious Optimism Prevails** Only 25% are "very comfortable" with fully AI-driven transactions, indicating gradual rather than revolutionary adoption timeline
- **Market Opportunity Window** Veterans United's survey reveals untapped demand for AI-powered real estate tools, particularly in mortgage and financial advisory services
- **Implementation Strategy Critical** Success will likely depend on hybrid models combining AI efficiency with human oversight rather than complete automation
