Real estate agents only clearly outperform AI on three of 23 home sale tasks—negotiation, hyperlocal knowledge, and emotional support—worth an estimated $2,000-$6,500 rather than the $12,000-$45,000 sellers currently pay in commissions. The Alloy Advisors report predicts the standard 5.44% commission model will collapse within 5-7 years as AI automation makes roughly half of current transaction costs "overpriced."

---

- **AI Dominance in Core Tasks** AI outperforms humans on 10 of 23 home sale tasks, including MLS listings, property descriptions, and scheduling, with market value approaching "near-zero" for information that agents once exclusively controlled

- **Human Value Concentration** Only three tasks show clear human advantage—negotiation execution, hyperlocal market knowledge, and emotional crisis management—but quality matters significantly, with low-volume agents losing even these advantages to AI

- **Commission Repricing Pressure** Current seller costs of $30,200 on a $400,000 home include $17,000-$22,000 in "overpriced" services, with the same human work costing $12,000 on modest homes versus $45,000 on luxury properties

- **Trust Gap Opportunity** While 65% of consumers trust AI for price comparisons, only 14% trust it to act on their behalf, creating market opportunity for hybrid human-AI advisory models rather than pure AI platforms

- **Business Model Timeline** The 5.44% commission standard will "break" within 5-7 years, though full-service agency won't disappear entirely, requiring immediate business model modifications for sustainability

- **Market Positioning Strategy** Success lies in the middle ground between pure AI platforms lacking consumer trust and incumbents ignoring change—products that use AI to enhance human judgment and transparency

- **Consumer Education Tool** New free calculator shows net proceeds across seven selling approaches (traditional, discount, FSBO, iBuyer, etc.) to help sellers understand alternatives beyond the current two-option mindset
