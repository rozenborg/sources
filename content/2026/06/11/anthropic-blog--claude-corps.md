Anthropic is launching Claude Corps, a $150 million national fellowship program that will train 1,000 early-career professionals over three years to work full-time with nonprofits across America, teaching AI skills while addressing economic disruption from transformative AI systems. The initiative represents a significant workforce development investment aimed at democratizing AI benefits beyond Silicon Valley, with fellows earning $85,000 annually plus benefits to spend 12 months helping organizations ranging from food banks to veteran services leverage Claude's capabilities.

---

- **Program Structure** Three-organization partnership with Anthropic funding and strategy, CodePath handling employment and training, and Social Finance managing evaluation and scaling mechanisms
- **Fellow Benefits** $85,000 salary plus benefits, intensive Claude training, weekly ongoing education, mentorship support, and generous AI token budgets for hands-on experience
- **Geographic Reach** At least 400 nonprofits across 70+ communities will host fellows, spanning diverse sectors from marine conservation in Florida to economic development in Arkansas heartland
- **Application Timeline** Rolling admissions starting now through July 2025 for October 2026 launch, open to anyone over 18 with under two years work experience regardless of educational background
- **Strategic Intent** Positions Anthropic as addressing AI workforce displacement proactively while creating skilled AI talent pipeline and demonstrating responsible AI deployment in underserved sectors
- **Scaling Ambition** Plans to open-source core technology infrastructure and expand internationally, suggesting this could become a template for industry-wide workforce transition programs
- **Measurement Focus** Rigorous evaluation of both nonprofit mission advancement and fellow skill development to refine the model for potential massive expansion beyond initial 1,000 participants
