Anthropic launches Claude Fable 5, its most capable AI model yet with state-of-the-art performance across software engineering, scientific research, and vision tasks, alongside Claude Mythos 5 for select cybersecurity and research partners. The company implements unprecedented safeguards that automatically route sensitive queries to less capable models, marking a new approach to safely deploying frontier AI capabilities while maintaining general availability.

---

- **Capability breakthrough** Fable 5 achieves state-of-the-art performance on nearly all benchmarks, with customers reporting months of engineering work compressed into days and autonomous completion of complex multi-step tasks previously requiring human oversight

- **Tiered safety architecture** New classifier system automatically routes cybersecurity, biology/chemistry, and model distillation queries to Claude Opus 4.8, triggering in less than 5% of sessions while maintaining robust jailbreak resistance through extensive red-team testing

- **Dual model strategy** Mythos 5 offers the same underlying capabilities as Fable 5 but with lifted safeguards for vetted cybersecurity partners and select researchers, expanding through government-coordinated trusted access programs

- **Scientific research acceleration** Mythos 5 demonstrates 10x speedup in drug design tasks, generates novel molecular biology hypotheses preferred by scientists 80% of the time, and autonomously conducted week-long genomics research outperforming published Science journal models

- **Enhanced data governance** Introduction of mandatory 30-day data retention for all Mythos-class model traffic to detect sophisticated attacks and jailbreak attempts, with privacy protections and automatic deletion protocols

- **Aggressive pricing strategy** Both models priced at $10/$50 per million input/output tokens, less than half the cost of previous Mythos Preview, with staged rollout managing capacity constraints through temporary subscription inclusion

- **Government collaboration expansion** Project Glasswing extends to 150+ organizations across 15+ countries, signaling deeper integration between AI companies and national security infrastructure for cyber defense applications
