The US government has issued an emergency directive forcing Anthropic to immediately suspend access to its Fable 5 and Mythos 5 AI models for all users, citing national security concerns over an alleged jailbreaking method that Anthropic claims demonstrates only minor vulnerabilities already discoverable by other publicly available models.

---

- **Emergency shutdown** US government directive at 5:21pm ET requires immediate suspension of Fable 5 and Mythos 5 access for all foreign nationals, effectively forcing complete service termination to ensure compliance
- **Disputed threat assessment** Government cites jailbreak method that can identify cybersecurity vulnerabilities, but Anthropic argues the technique only reveals minor, previously known flaws discoverable by other public models including OpenAI's GPT-5.5
- **Extensive pre-launch testing** Anthropic conducted thousands of hours of red-teaming with US government, UK AISI, and third-party organizations, finding no universal jailbreaks that could broadly bypass safeguards
- **Defense-in-depth strategy** Company implemented 30-day data retention policy and monitoring systems to quickly detect attacks, acknowledging perfect jailbreak resistance is currently impossible across the industry
- **Industry-wide implications** Anthropic warns that applying this recall standard universally would halt all new frontier model deployments, as similar vulnerabilities exist in competing systems
- **Process criticism** Company argues government action lacks transparency and technical grounding, calling for statutory processes to govern model deployment decisions
- **Customer impact** Hundreds of millions of users lose access to two key models while other Anthropic models remain operational
