Anthropic's technical analysis reveals that recursive self-improvement is not a distant sci-fi scenario but an engineering pipeline already under construction, with Claude-authored code rising from single digits to over 80% of merged code at Anthropic between February 2025 and May 2026. The critical shift is from "Can AI help researchers?" to "Which parts of AI development still require humans?" as coding agents demonstrate they can debug complex training failures, run multi-hour autonomous experiments, and increasingly handle the implementation bottlenecks that constrain frontier model development.

---

- **Operational RSI Loop Already Emerging** - Modern AI labs use a six-component feedback system: objective setting, change generation, execution infrastructure, evaluation, promotion mechanisms, and oversight controls, with agents now handling substantial portions of coding, debugging, and experiment execution.

- **Bottleneck Migration Over Time** - Following Amdahl's law, as AI accelerates coding and experimentation, human review and judgment become the constraining factors, forcing organizations to either automate verification or accept reduced oversight depth as agent-generated work volume explodes.

- **Code Generation as Infrastructure Control** - AI systems that can manipulate training orchestration, data pipelines, evaluation harnesses, and deployment code effectively participate in their own development cycle, with Claude reportedly achieving 52x speedups on model optimization tasks by April 2026.

- **Virtual Lab Architecture** - The most plausible RSI path involves networked specialized agents handling hypothesis generation, implementation, auditing, and verification within shared memory systems, rather than single monolithic models working in isolation.

- **Control Requires Separation** - Safe recursive development demands independent verification systems where models under evaluation cannot control their own certification pipeline, including automated review agents, interpretability checks, and tamper-evident logging.

- **Compute as Governor and Accelerator** - Access controls on training clusters, inference resources, and sensitive data provide practical throttles on recursive loops, while compute availability increasingly determines the clock speed of AI research rather than human labor.

- **Three Futures Framework** - Current trajectory points toward sustained acceleration in AI-assisted development with humans retaining direction-setting, though full autonomous successor-building remains uncertain and depends on whether models develop reliable research judgment beyond execution capability.

- **Critical Indicators to Monitor** - Watch for agents completing longer autonomous tasks, debugging without human intervention, proposing research directions that survive expert review, and designing evaluations that remain useful after models learn to game existing benchmarks.
