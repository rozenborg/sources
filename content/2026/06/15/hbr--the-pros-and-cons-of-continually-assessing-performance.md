Organizations must abandon static job descriptions and traditional credentials in favor of real-time capability tracking as AI fundamentally reshapes which tasks humans and machines perform best. The aviation industry's shift from simple flight hours to continuous competency assessment offers a proven model for workforce adaptation in an AI-driven economy.

---

- **Skills decay urgency** Traditional credentials become obsolete faster as AI automates core job functions, making continuous assessment essential for maintaining workforce relevance
- **Aviation precedent** Airlines successfully transitioned from hour-based pilot certification to ongoing competency evaluation, demonstrating feasibility of dynamic assessment systems
- **Dynamic labor division** AI's rapid evolution means the human-machine task allocation changes constantly, requiring real-time visibility into what employees can actually do versus their job titles
- **Competitive advantage** Organizations with granular, current capability data can redeploy talent more effectively than competitors relying on outdated role definitions
- **Implementation risk** Continuous assessment systems require significant technology investment and cultural change management to avoid employee resistance and privacy concerns
- **Strategic workforce planning** Real-time capability tracking enables predictive identification of skill gaps before they impact operations, allowing proactive training investments
