## Summary

## Key Facts

- AI startups increasingly keeping teams small while targeting billion-dollar outcomes through high revenue-per-employee ratios
- 50% of AI agent tool calls on Anthropic's API are for software engineering; 16 other verticals each represent less than 9% of usage
- Agent session lengths and complexity increasing over time, indicating growing user trust
- Wall Street Journal tracking pattern of AI companies using automation to avoid traditional headcount scaling
- 3,500 high school girls attended SheTech Explorer Day, showing strong AI engagement despite research indicating slower female adoption rates

## Strategic Implications

- Software engineering agent market approaching saturation while most other verticals remain wide open
- "Small by design" positioning becoming competitive advantage for AI startups seeking investor attention
- Revenue-per-employee and operating leverage metrics gaining equal importance to raw growth metrics
- Key-person risk and system fragility emerging as critical vulnerabilities for lean AI teams
- Vertical-specific AI agents with domain expertise represent largest untapped opportunity

## Actionable Insights

- **For founders**: Target non-engineering verticals (healthcare, legal, finance, education) where agent adoption remains below 9%
- **Investment strategy**: Focus on companies demonstrating high operating leverage through AI automation rather than traditional scaling
- **Product development**: Design deeper, higher-stakes automations with robust monitoring and guardrails as user trust increases
- **Risk management**: Implement documentation, ownership boundaries, and succession planning to counter small-team fragility
- **Market positioning**: Frame "staying small" as efficiency signal rather than resource constraint when fundraising
- **Technical focus**: Build agents that handle end-to-end processes with proprietary data and domain-specific context, not just text/code generation
