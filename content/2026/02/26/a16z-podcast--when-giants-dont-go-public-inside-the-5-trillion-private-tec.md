## Summary

## Key Facts

- Private tech market cap has reached $5 trillion, representing 10x growth over the past decade
- Traditional IPO timeline has extended significantly, with companies staying private longer
- AI companies are potentially accelerating their path to public markets ("speed-running")
- Legacy software company valuations have collapsed
- Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs) and tender offers are increasingly used as liquidity mechanisms

## Strategic Implications

- $5 trillion locked in private markets creates significant liquidity constraints for employees and early investors
- Extended private market stays concentrate wealth among institutional investors with private market access
- Retail investors face reduced access to high-growth tech companies during their fastest expansion phases
- AI sector may break the trend of prolonged private ownership due to unique characteristics
- Legacy software devaluation signals potential market rotation toward next-generation technologies

## Actionable Insights

- Founders should evaluate alternative liquidity mechanisms (SPVs, tender offers) to retain talent and provide employee liquidity
- Investors need private market exposure strategies to access high-growth tech opportunities
- Companies should assess whether AI-driven business models warrant accelerated public market timelines
- Legacy software companies must demonstrate differentiation or risk continued valuation pressure
- Market participants should monitor AI company IPO patterns as potential indicators of broader market timing shifts
- Employees at private tech companies should understand and negotiate for liquidity options beyond traditional equity compensation
