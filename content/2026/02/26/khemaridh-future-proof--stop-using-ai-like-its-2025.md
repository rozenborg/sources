## Summary

- AI landscape shifted dramatically from 2025 to 2026, moving away from chat-based interfaces toward outcome-driven products

- Model differentiation has largely disappeared - top AI models now perform similarly with minimal competitive advantages

- Key product evolution: Claude Code (coding), Claude Cowork (knowledge work automation), Excel/PowerPoint plugins, NotebookLM (retrieval + presentations), ChatGPT (lagging behind)

- "The Harness" concept: Different AI products have varying risk/capability levels - chat (car seat), Cowork (booster seat), Claude Code (sports car seatbelt)

- Copy-paste AI workflows becoming obsolete as products integrate directly into work environments

- Shift from "answers" (Google Maps model) to "outcomes" (Waymo model) - users specify end goals rather than step-by-step instructions

- White collar job displacement warnings (Citrini research, 2028 predictions) causing market volatility and concern

- Counter-argument: Human adaptability and creativity will drive evolution rather than replacement

- Non-technical workers already building automation tools for research, business development, and recruiting

- Strategic recommendation: Focus on "which product surface to use" rather than "which AI model to choose"

- Actionable shift: Move from prompt engineering and model selection to outcome-based AI tool selection

- Skills evolution: From copy-paste proficiency to domain expertise codification and tool integration

- Market opportunity: Training programs showing success in upskilling workers for AI-augmented roles
