## Summary

- Anthropic acquired Vercept to enhance Claude's computer use capabilities for multi-step tasks in live applications

- Vercept team includes AI perception experts Kiana Ehsani, Luca Weihs, and Ross Girshick with deep experience in AI-software interaction

- Vercept will shut down external products and integrate fully into Anthropic's development efforts

- Claude Sonnet 4.6 achieved 72.5% performance on OSWorld computer use benchmark, up from under 15% in late 2024

- Current capabilities include navigating complex spreadsheets and completing web forms across browser tabs at near-human levels

- Computer use enables Claude to perform tasks impossible through code alone by operating directly within live applications

- Acquisition follows previous purchase of Bun, indicating pattern of strategic team acquisitions

- Target use cases include: writing/running code across repositories, synthesizing research from multiple sources, managing cross-tool workflows

- Strategic focus on AI systems that can "see and act within the same software humans use daily"

- Acquisition criteria emphasize technical ambition, capability advancement, and alignment with safety principles

- Company actively recruiting individual engineers beyond team acquisitions
