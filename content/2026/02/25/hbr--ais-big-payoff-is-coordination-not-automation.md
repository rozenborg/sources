## Summary

**Key Facts**
- Traditional AI economic analysis focuses on reduced prediction costs and creation costs
- Translation cost reduction represents the most significant but least understood AI economic impact
- Translation costs encompass converting information between different formats, languages, and contexts

**Strategic Implications**
- Organizations relying solely on automation and prediction improvements may miss AI's largest value opportunity
- Competitive advantage will increasingly come from superior coordination capabilities rather than just efficiency gains
- Cross-functional collaboration barriers will diminish as AI handles translation between different business domains and technical systems

**Actionable Insights**
- Audit current translation bottlenecks across departments, systems, and stakeholder communications
- Invest in AI solutions that bridge gaps between different data formats, business units, and external partners
- Develop organizational capabilities around AI-enabled coordination rather than focusing exclusively on task automation
- Identify opportunities where improved translation could unlock network effects and ecosystem value
- Train teams to recognize and leverage translation-based AI applications in their specific domains
