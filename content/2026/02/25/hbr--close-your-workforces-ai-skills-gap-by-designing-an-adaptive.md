## Summary

## Key Facts

- 68% of leaders/employees believe they can keep pace with AI, yet 93% report workforce barriers (underdeveloped skills, inadequate training) limit progress
- Organizations mistake momentum for progress - tools roll out but necessary capabilities remain underdeveloped
- Traditional ROI models fail due to AI's speed and unpredictable value emergence
- Agentic AI's value comes from automating routine tasks within roles rather than replacing entire roles

## Strategic Implications

- Speed of AI adaptation demands organizational redesign, not just tool implementation
- Leadership paradigm must shift from expertise-based control to exploration-based guidance
- Human advantage lies in orchestrating AI adoption, interpretation, and refinement rather than competing with AI capabilities
- Value creation requires portfolio approach balancing specific use cases with broader AI enablement

## Actionable Insights

### Leadership Development
- Invest in real-world learning, executive coaching, and cross-functional collaboration over commoditized training
- Prioritize curiosity over certainty, sense-making over quick solutions
- Embed learning into actual decision-making processes

### Workforce Integration
- Regularly reassess task division: what AI handles today, what's automatable in 18-24 months, what reshapes roles long-term
- Simplify workflows before automating them to avoid scaling broken processes
- Redefine job responsibilities to prioritize owning AI outcomes and reward through advancement
- Enable experimentation with hands-on training, guardrails, and feedback loops

### Value Creation Strategy
- Use rough estimates, small tests, and range-based measurements instead of fixed business cases
- Assess readiness factors (data, architecture, governance) before implementation
- Prioritize viable use cases over novel but unrealistic applications
- Create step-level guardrails throughout agentic processes
- Build balanced portfolio: AI solutions for specific use cases (early returns) + broader enablement (compound value over time)
