## Summary

## Key Facts

- CoStar reported $900M Q4 revenue (27% YoY growth) and $3.25B full-year revenue (19% YoY growth)
- 59th consecutive quarter of double-digit year-over-year growth
- Net income rebounded to $47M in Q4 after $31M Q3 loss
- Homes.com Network has 31,000 agent subscribers generating ~$100M annualized revenue
- CoStar sites reached 139M average monthly unique visitors in Q4
- Cash reserves dropped from $4.68B to $1.63B due to acquisitions and $500M share buyback
- Company eliminated ~$120M in duplicative costs post-Matterport acquisition

## Strategic Implications

- Activist investors Third Point and D.E. Shaw pressuring CoStar to abandon Homes.com despite management's confidence
- Homes AI launch represents major strategic pivot toward AI-powered search experiences
- CoStar successfully captured 1,200 apartment properties from Redfin after its Zillow partnership
- Company positioning against "lead shotgunning" tactics used by competitors like Zillow
- Management believes current AI interface will make traditional website navigation obsolete within a year

- **Monitor AI adoption metrics**: Track user engagement and lead conversion rates from Homes AI to validate strategic direction
