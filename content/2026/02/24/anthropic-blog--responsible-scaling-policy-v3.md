## Summary

## Key Facts

- Anthropic released Responsible Scaling Policy (RSP) Version 3.0 after 2+ years of implementation experience
- Original RSP launched September 2023 when LLMs were primarily chat interfaces; now they can browse web, write code, use computers, and take autonomous actions
- ASL-3 safeguards successfully activated in May 2025 for biological/chemical weapon prevention
- Policy uses conditional "if-then" commitments: if models exceed capability thresholds, then stricter safeguards are implemented
- OpenAI and Google DeepMind adopted similar frameworks within months of Anthropic's original RSP announcement

## Strategic Implications

- **Capability evaluation challenges**: Pre-set thresholds create "zones of ambiguity" where it's unclear if models have definitively crossed safety boundaries
- **Government engagement lag**: Despite rapid AI advances, federal safety policy moves slowly with current focus shifted toward competitiveness over safety
- **Unilateral limitations**: Higher-level safeguards (ASL-4/5) may be impossible to implement without industry-wide coordination or government support
- **Industry influence**: RSP principles are informing AI legislation in California (SB 53), New York (RAISE Act), and EU AI Act
- **Scaling constraints**: Current approach faces structural challenges as capabilities advance beyond what individual companies can safely manage alone

## Actionable Insights

- **Dual-track approach**: New RSP separates achievable unilateral company commitments from industry-wide recommendations for comprehensive risk management
- **Transparency mechanisms**: 
  - Frontier Safety Roadmap with public goals and progress grading (non-binding but publicly declared)
  - Risk Reports published every 3-6 months with detailed safety profiles
  - External expert review requirement for high-risk models
- **Operational priorities**:
  - Launch "moonshot R&D" for unprecedented information security levels
  - Develop automated red-teaming surpassing current bug bounty contributions  
  - Implement systematic measures for constitutional AI behavior
  - Establish comprehensive AI development activity monitoring
  - Publish regulatory ladder proposals for scalable government policy
- **Risk mitigation focus**: Emphasize realistic, achievable safeguards while maintaining comprehensive industry risk mapping
- **Policy engagement**: Continue evidence-based advocacy for government AI safety frameworks despite current political climate favoring competitiveness
