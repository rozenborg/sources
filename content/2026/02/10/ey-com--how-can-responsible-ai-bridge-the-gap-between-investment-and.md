## Summary

- **Most companies have implemented 7 out of 10 responsible AI measures on average**, indicating widespread but incomplete adoption

- **Three-stage responsible AI journey**: Communication (articulating principles) → Execution (controls, KPIs, training) → Governance (committees, audits)

- **TMT sector leads adoption** with higher dependency on technology driving more comprehensive implementation:
  - 80% communicate principles externally vs. 71% other industries
  - 74% have oversight committees vs. 61% other industries  
  - 72% conduct independent assessments vs. 61% other industries

- **Key implementation gap**: Companies strong on principle communication but weaker on governance and oversight mechanisms

- **Strategic implication**: Technology-dependent sectors face higher regulatory and reputational risks, driving faster responsible AI adoption

- **Actionable insight**: Establish formal governance structures (committees, audits) to move beyond principle statements toward measurable accountability

- **Investment priority**: Focus on execution stage elements (controls, KPIs, workforce training) to bridge principle-to-practice gap

- **Competitive advantage**: Early responsible AI adoption in non-TMT sectors presents differentiation opportunity given lower industry averages
