## Summary

## Key Facts

- Companies are heavily investing in digital analytics hubs with AI/generative AI capabilities
- Enterprise CRM systems and marketing technology platforms are primary investment areas
- Commercial functions receive the highest concentration of digital investment due to their direct impact on growth and customers
- Despite significant investments, companies are not realizing expected value from these digital initiatives

## Strategic Implications

- Current digital investment strategy may be misaligned with value creation objectives
- Commercial functions are viewed as highest priority for digital transformation due to revenue impact
- Gap exists between digital investment levels and actual business value delivery
- Organizations may be over-investing in technology without proper implementation or adoption strategies

## Actionable Insights

- Audit current digital investments to identify value gaps and underperforming initiatives
- Establish clear ROI metrics and measurement frameworks before making additional digital investments
- Review implementation and change management processes for existing digital tools
- Prioritize user adoption and training programs to maximize value from current technology investments
- Consider consolidating or optimizing existing digital platforms before adding new capabilities
- Align digital investment decisions with specific business outcomes rather than technology features
