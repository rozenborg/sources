## Summary

- **Partnership Details**: Anthropic partnering with CodePath to redesign coding curriculum around Claude and Claude Code for 20,000+ students at community colleges, state schools, and HBCUs

- **Target Demographics**: Over 40% of CodePath students from families earning under $50,000/year, addressing AI education inequality at historically overlooked institutions

- **Curriculum Integration**: Claude integrated into three AI courses (Foundations of AI Engineering, Applications of AI Engineering, AI Open-Source Capstone) starting fall 2025

- **Pilot Results**: 100+ students successfully used Claude Code on real open-source projects (GitLab, Puter, Dokploy) with positive learning outcomes, particularly for students with limited programming language experience

- **Academic Credit Milestone**: Howard University offering first university-credit course using CodePath's applied AI curriculum in partnership with Thurgood Marshall College Fund

- **Accelerated Learning Claims**: CodePath CEO states technology enables teaching "in two years what used to take four," positioning AI-assisted development as standard for entry-level engineering roles

- **Research Component**: Joint public research initiative to study AI's impact on coding education and economic opportunity dynamics

- **Global Education Strategy**: Partnership expands Anthropic's education initiatives including American Federation of Teachers (1.8M members), Iceland national pilot, Rwanda learning companion, and White House AI education pledge

- **Market Position**: Anthropic positioned as "market leader in enterprise AI and coding" with $14B run-rate revenue growing 10x annually

- **Strategic Implication**: Democratizing access to frontier AI tools in education to influence who participates in shaping the AI economy
