## Summary

## Key Facts

- Waymo raised $16 billion in February 2026 at $126 billion valuation, led by Dragoneer, DST Global, and Sequoia Capital
- Currently operates fully driverless commercial service in 5 U.S. cities: Phoenix, San Francisco, Los Angeles, Austin, and Atlanta
- Completed 20 million rides total; tripled annual rides to 15 million in 2025; provides 400,000+ weekly trips across 6 metro areas
- Claims 90% lower serious collision rate compared to human drivers
- Admits to using remote human operators in Philippines for difficult situations assistance
- Recalled 3,067 vehicles in December 2026 for illegally passing school buses

## Product Announcements & Technical Capabilities

- **Ojai Vehicle Launch (January 2026)**: New Zeekr-built electric robotaxi with no steering wheel/pedals, 5-passenger capacity, automated cleaning systems
- **6th-generation hardware**: 13 cameras, 4 LiDAR sensors, 6 radar sensors with 500-meter range in all weather
- **Freeway capability**: First U.S. company offering fully driverless freeway rides (November 2025)
- **All-electric fleet**: Transitioned from Chrysler Pacifica to Jaguar I-PACE vehicles (May 2023)
- **24/7 airport service**: Available at Phoenix Sky Harbor and San Jose Mineta airports
- **Weather adaptation**: Specialized sensor suite for winter conditions and salt spray resistance

## Strategic Implications

- **Market dominance strategy**: Rapid expansion represents land grab to secure rider loyalty before competitors enter market
- **International expansion**: First overseas operations planned in London and Tokyo for 2026
- **Partnership leverage**: Deep Uber integration in Austin/Atlanta provides instant access to massive customer base
- **Premium positioning**: 30-40% price premium over traditional rideshare ($11/km vs $8/km) positions as luxury alternative
- **Safety differentiation**: Markets no-human-driver safety benefit, particularly for women and children transport
- **Technical moat**: Trained on millions of real-world miles plus billions of simulated scenarios through Carcraft platform

## Actionable Insights

- **2026 expansion targets**: 20+ new cities including southern wave (Miami, Dallas, Houston, San Antonio, Orlando)
- **Ambitious growth goal**: Target 1 million rides per week by end of 2026
- **Operational scaling**: Uber partnership model for cleaning/maintenance reduces operational overhead
- **Regulatory navigation**: Congressional scrutiny over remote operators and cybersecurity vulnerabilities requires proactive compliance
- **Fleet transition**: Moving from retrofitted vehicles to purpose-built robotaxis improves passenger experience and operational efficiency
- **Winter weather testing**: Detroit and Washington D.C. trials address critical technical gap for northern market expansion
