## Summary

- **Unprecedented capital intensity**: AI investment cycle requires orders of magnitude more capital than previous technology waves, fundamentally changing venture capital dynamics

- **Blurred investment boundaries**: Traditional distinctions between venture/growth stages and apps/infrastructure categories are dissolving in AI markets

- **Top-heavy funding distribution**: Frontier model companies (OpenAI, Anthropic) raising more aggregate capital than entire ecosystems built on their platforms

- **Capital flywheel dynamics**: Success in AI creates compounding advantages where early winners can reinvest massive resources to maintain technological leads

- **Perception-reality gap widening**: Industry experiencing largest disconnect between public hype/expectations and actual deployment/revenue generation in tech history

- **Infrastructure-first value capture**: Unlike previous cycles, foundational AI companies capturing disproportionate value before application layer matures

- **New investment thesis required**: Traditional VC metrics and timelines inadequate for evaluating AI companies requiring billion-dollar compute investments

- **Strategic consolidation inevitable**: Capital requirements will likely force industry consolidation around few well-funded frontier model providers

- **Application layer squeeze**: Companies building on top of AI models face compressed margins and uncertain differentiation as foundation models improve

- **Long-term competitive moats unclear**: Uncertainty whether sustainable advantages will emerge at model layer or application layer over multi-year timeframes
