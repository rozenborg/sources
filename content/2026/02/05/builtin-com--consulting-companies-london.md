## Summary

- London's consulting sector revenue increased 66% over five years with nearly double the job growth in same period

- Financial services, technology, and public sector consulting are the three dominant practice areas in London

- "Big Four" firms (Deloitte, PwC, KPMG, EY) control majority of consulting business volume in the city

- Cybersecurity consulting focusing on email security and human risk management gaining traction (Mimecast using AI for threat protection)

- Process mining and data analytics consulting emerging as high-growth niche (Celonis opened London office in 2019 with 200+ specialists)

- AI and data engineering consulting becoming core differentiator for boutique firms (Sigmoid, MARGO specializing in complex IT ecosystems)

- Digital transformation consulting shifting toward "smartsourcing" models to optimize labor and supply costs

- E-commerce consulting expanding beyond traditional models into marketplace, dropship, and ad buying optimization

- Healthcare technology consulting requiring dual expertise in medical science and business technology

- Freelancer consulting platforms scaling across European markets, indicating shift toward flexible workforce models

- Financial services consulting increasingly focused on legacy system migration and regulatory compliance

- Enterprise resource planning consulting concentrated within specific software ecosystems (Zone & Co in NetSuite)

- Risk and compliance consulting integrating software tools with managed services for comprehensive governance solutions
