## Summary

**Katherine Boyle's Core Thesis:**
- American Dynamism practice at a16z was controversial when launched 4 years ago - simply saying "America" out loud was stunning to Silicon Valley in 2022
- Russia's invasion of Ukraine fundamentally changed investment landscape and Silicon Valley's relationship with defense/national security
- Focus on investing in companies that explicitly support American national interests

**Strategic Shifts in Defense Investment:**
- Traditional venture capital avoided defense sector due to regulatory complexity and long sales cycles
- Ukraine conflict demonstrated critical importance of defense technology innovation
- Silicon Valley now more receptive to dual-use technologies with national security applications
- Defense spending increasingly flowing to startups rather than traditional defense contractors

**Investment Approach Evolution:**
- Targeting companies with both commercial viability and national security relevance
- Emphasis on technologies that can scale rapidly and serve multiple markets
- Recognition that geopolitical competition requires private sector innovation capacity

**Market Implications:**
- 25-year investment horizon suggests long-term commitment to defense innovation
- Increasing convergence between commercial technology and national security needs
- Venture capital becoming key driver of defense modernization
- Shift from purely profit-driven to mission-aligned investment strategies

**Actionable Investment Themes:**
- Dual-use technologies with commercial and defense applications
- Companies addressing critical supply chain vulnerabilities
- Technologies enabling rapid manufacturing and deployment capabilities
- Infrastructure supporting American technological independence
