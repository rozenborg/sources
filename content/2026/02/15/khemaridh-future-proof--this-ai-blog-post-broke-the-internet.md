## Summary

- **80+ million views** on Matt Shumer's "Something Big is Happening" AI post drove viral panic among executives, influencers, and media

- **Major corporations reporting AI coding dominance**: Spotify's best developers haven't written code since December 2026, significant GitHub activity now AI-generated

- **AI excels at software problems** due to verifiable outputs and narrow language requirements, making coding workflows easily automatable

- **Non-software industries face adoption barriers**: limited data access, proprietary datasets, and difficulty converting complex processes into verifiable software problems

- **Screen-based jobs under threat** but domain expertise remains critical differentiator - AI fluency alone insufficient without industry knowledge

- **Investment decisions example**: Converting "Should I invest in Google?" into software requires identifying tens of thousands of variables and clarifying ambiguous parameters

- **Finance, healthcare, law industries resistant** due to legacy tools (Excel), data sensitivity, and verification challenges outside coding

- **Domain expertise acquisition model**: "Books vs Reps" - AI accelerates learning component but real-world experience ("reps") cannot be speed-run

- **Strategic opportunity**: Combination of AI fluency + deep domain expertise creates "nasty combo" competitive advantage

- **Actionable insight**: Transform existing workflows into software problems while building irreplaceable domain experience through hands-on practice

- **Timeline urgency**: AI disruption already underway, not future threat - immediate skill development required

- **Coding accessibility**: $100/month Claude subscription makes high-quality software development accessible to non-programmers
