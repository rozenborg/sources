## Summary

- EY offers "Four Futures sessions" - strategic planning exercise projecting tax functions to 2030, then working backward to identify current-day preparation steps

- EY.ai Tax Lab provides full-day hands-on sessions where tax teams build actual AI agents rather than just strategic plans

- Common AI agent use cases emerging from labs: knowledge/policy assistants for business questions, horizon-scanning agents for regulatory monitoring, document analysis for VAT contract/invoice processing

- EY's "AI Factory model" addresses organizations with 10-20+ scattered GenAI initiatives by providing structured governance, reusable prompts, and portfolio-level ROI assessment

- **Strategic Challenge**: Tax functions trapped in short-term experimentation cycles, jumping between AI tools without sustainable strategy

- **2030 Vision**: Small, agile tax teams supported by robust AI systems enabling real-time analysis, automated repetitive processes, and deeper business integration

- **Transformation Barrier**: Lack of AI skills identified as second-biggest adoption obstacle after data issues

- **Employee Journey**: Magic phase (initial enthusiasm) → disappointment phase (discovering AI limitations) → purposeful use phase (understanding appropriate applications)

- **Skill Evolution**: Data/technology skills now considered more important than pure technical tax depth for future tax professionals

- **Implementation Framework**: "Mindset, skillset, toolset" approach requiring equal investment in human development and technology

- **Organizational Changes**: Leading companies appointing AI champions, hiring diverse profiles (tax specialists with data skills, data analysts interested in tax), providing bias/ethics training beyond tool functionality

- **Risk Management**: Without proper guidance, initial AI enthusiasm degrades into occasional use by small enthusiast groups rather than systematic adoption
